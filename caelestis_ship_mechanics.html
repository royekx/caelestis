Ship Mechanics

Everything you need to know about flying, fighting, and surviving in Wildspace.

Your Ship
Armor Class
What attackers need to hit the ship. Same as creature AC.
Hull Points
The ship's health. Reaches 0 and the ship stops functioning. Hard to repair mid-combat.
Bulwark Points
A damage buffer in front of Hull Points. Damage hits BP first. Easier to recover than HP.
Speed
How far the ship moves per turn in combat (in feet).
Agility
How far the ship can turn per turn, in degrees. Higher = more maneuverable.
Crew
How many sailors are aboard. They man the weapons — fewer crew means fewer attacks.
How Bulwark Works All incoming damage depletes Bulwark Points first. Hull only takes damage once BP reaches 0. Your Boatswain passively restores BP each turn — but a Fire critical effect stops that recovery until they put it out.

Combat
Your initiative. You roll and act normally, same as any combat. Your character's actions and abilities all still work.
Your role. Your bridge crew role gives you additional special actions on top of your normal ones — not instead of them. See the Crew Roles reference.
The ship's turn. The ship acts at the Spelljammer's initiative. They direct movement, turning, and weapon fire without spending their own actions.
Switching roles. You can switch to a vacant role as a bonus action. Single-occupant roles (Spelljammer, Captain) require the current occupant to agree.
What You Can Target
The Ship

Deals damage to BP then Hull. The direct approach.

Weapons

Destroy a weapon to silence it. Each has its own HP.

Crew

Fewer crew = fewer weapon attacks the ship can make.

Officers

Individually tracked enemies. Taking them out has big impact.

Ship Weapon Attacks
d20 + Proficiency + Str or Dex
Your choice of Strength or Dexterity modifier on the attack roll.
Damage — no modifier
Roll the weapon's damage dice only. Your ability modifier is not added to damage.
Crew requirement. Each weapon needs a minimum number of crew to fire. No crew available = can't fire. Gunners at Officer rank reduce this by 1.
One fire per turn. A weapon can only be fired once per turn. A crew member can only man one weapon per turn.
Class features. Features that apply to weapon attacks (Extra Attack, Divine Smite, etc.) can apply to ship weapons — but only once you reach Officer rank as a Gunner. Check with your DM for specifics.

Boarding
Within 5 ft. Just move across. No action required — treat it like stepping through a doorway.
Grappling hook or plank. Either requires an action to set up, then you can cross.
Jump, fly, teleport. Any movement option available to your character works normally. Use the Ready action if you're waiting for the Spelljammer to close distance.
Captain's Boarding Party. At Officer rank, the Captain can call a coordinated boarding action — everyone who joins swings across simultaneously at the start of the next round.
✦ ✦ ✦
Hazards
What they are. Non-combat challenges the ship faces — asteroid clusters, interstellar fog, fire on board, crew conflicts, electrical fields. Each is a group skill check.
How they work. Each character contributes one check. Your bridge crew role shapes which options are available to you. The crew also makes a single roll. Majority success or failure determines the outcome — four tiers from Total Success to Total Failure.
Be creative. The listed checks are suggestions. If you have a better idea for how your character would help, pitch it — the DM will call for an appropriate roll.
