Previous Session

Orientation threw five strangers into a simulation they couldn't win, then put them through a full day of academy life — bunks, Sky Dock, the Spelljammer Nexus, the Gymnasium. A simple delivery errand to Mirt the Merciless ended in a creature attack in his quarters: a coordinated diversion while the academy was being robbed. Sol chose to preserve the last creature alive rather than kill it. Casey slipped alone into Mirt's wardrobe and heard a voice say Go there. Gregory felt something directional pulling at his core. Tumak is hiding the constellation shirt Casey passed him. Boogie pulled a half-missing model ship from inside his own chest.

Voyage 002 opens the morning after. For the first time since arriving, the day briefly opens before the academy calls them back in.

Main Plot This Session

Morning — Bartholomew is assigned to this crew by Tarto and pointed to the Weeping Goddess. He arrives before the party, meets Miken at the only open table. The crew filters in; the only open seats are there. Afternoon — Free roam. Let them choose: Nexus, Sky Dock, Stores, Quarters. The academy runs visibly strained at the edges. End of Day — Tarto recalls the crew to recount the Mirt incident. The preserved creature matters; Sol's choice is validated; the theft plot sharpens. Montage — A few days pass. Show the academy feeling lived-in before the next mission. End on the debrief, the evening close, the montage, or the opening beat of Trial by Fire.

Strong Start — Bartholomew & Tarto
Read Aloud — Open on Bartholomew

The Boatswain's post is quieter than the rest of the academy. She doesn't greet you when you enter — she finishes the line she's writing, sets the ledger aside, and looks at you with the kind of attention that measures without announcing itself. "You're a wilder." Not a question. "That changes nothing once you're aboard a crew. There's one you'll be training with." She names the Weeping Goddess and the lunch hour without looking up again. "Find them. Eat. Keep up." The conversation is over before you realize it started.

Officer hallway, debrief room, or examination area near Mirt's quarters
What's Happening
Tarto recalls the crew. They recount the incident. The preserved creature is examined. Officers confirm it connects to broader, coordinated thefts. The crew is dismissed for the evening with more questions than answers.
DM Note — Sol's Moment

Make Sol's choice explicit and consequential. The officers learned something concrete because of him. Name it. Don't let it pass as routine.

Secrets & Clues
The academy thefts are targeted and coordinated, not opportunistic. Someone knew which rooms to hit and when to create a diversion.
Sol preserving the creature is the only reason the officers can learn anything concrete. If he'd killed it, the investigation would have nothing.
Fleet sometimes moves specialized personnel to field assignments, not just cargo. Do not reveal Ezra specifically — only imply this.
A deeper layer of academy secrecy exists above cadet clearance. Words like Watcher, Zenith, Monolith may surface only in fragments — overheard, never explained.
Something about Sol subtly distorts timing and probability — inconveniently, awkwardly, but usefully. The misfortune has a shape.

Miscellaneous DM Notes

Sol's minor misfortune must be inconvenient, awkward, and useful — not flashy. It reveals something. It does not embarrass him.

Let the academy feel busy and inhabited: drills, meals, passing cadets, dock work, and only occasional tension at the edges.
