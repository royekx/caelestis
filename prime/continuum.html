Layer 01·Cosmology·The Continuum

Continuum

What is true, and what runs whether anyone is watching. The structural rules underneath every world, every faction, every player choice. This layer is the cosmology on its own terms — what it does, how it behaves, and what it does not need anyone's permission to keep doing. The Campaign layer says what current beings are trying to do with it; the World layer says where it lives.

Running the Cosmology

How to wield this layer at the table. The guardrails that don't bend, the areas left deliberately open, and the questions to run any new situation through. Read this first; the cosmology itself follows.

Canon guardrails

Fixed unless intentionally revised. When the table presses on a thing, these are the answers reality has already chosen.

Cosmology
  • Existence is not an Axiom. Existence is Continuity — the emergent product of the Four Axioms in equilibrium. Both names refer to the same phenomenon.
  • The Four Axioms are Convergence, Dissolution, Boundary, Transformation. They are sentient, but constrained entirely by their function.
  • Monoliths do not cause Zeniths. They respond to them.
  • Zeniths are not always apocalyptic.
  • Mortal-made Zeniths are often the most unstable because they are forced.
  • Destroying a Monolith is not a clean solution. It risks archival failure and deeper loss. (The Fonains case is exceptional, not a template.)
  • The gods did not create the Continuum and do not have full mastery of it.
Factions
  • The Order of the All-Father knows more than most factions, but still operates with incomplete understanding.
  • Watchers are the Order's field arm — a known, respected partner organization, not ominous, not omnipotent world-police. The Fleet works with them routinely. Their internals are discreet; their existence is not secret.
  • Restorers are a hidden, independent faction pursuing the Mother of the Universe for their own restorative agenda. They are not Watcher partners or ordinary party allies.
Beings inside the system
  • No mortal, god, or other being created the Continuum's machinery — it precedes them. Beings act inside it.
  • Antagonists who attempt to manipulate Continuum-scale phenomena are working with a system, not against it — and the system was running long before they arrived.
  • The current antagonist's advantage is perspective, not power: he has stepped back far enough to see the system as a pattern. Full plan and motive live in The Remaking.
  • Monolith-based exploitation is one path to Continuum-scale influence. Other paths exist. Vecna found this one.

Flexible areas

Deliberately left broad so the campaign can adapt without contradiction:

  • The exact internal politics of the Bridge.
  • The full roster and hierarchy of the Order's field arm and the Restorers.
  • Which worlds or modules become central in the sandbox arc.
  • How much the public, the gods, or individual allied leaders know at any given moment.
  • Which Monolith events are natural and which were manipulated.
  • Whether Vecna's hypothesis about Continuity-strain (vs. Convergence-strain) is accurate.
  • The full shape of what an Axiom Fracture would look like in practice, if one occurred.

The table test

When introducing a new location, crisis, faction, or artifact, run it through these. If they're answerable, the campaign stays aligned with its own cosmology even when the players go somewhere unexpected.

  1. Is this event building toward, reacting to, or recovering from a threshold?
  2. Which faction notices it first, and why?
  3. Which Axiom's pressure is in play — Convergence, Dissolution, Boundary, or Transformation?
  4. Would the antagonist benefit if everyone misread this as a local problem?
  5. What would a Watcher do here?
  6. If a hidden Restorer interest exists here, what would it want?
  7. What part of Continuity is actually at stake beneath the surface story?
Core truth Exploration and cosmic wonder sit directly beside structural peril. The stars are beautiful, but beauty does not imply safety. Worlds are worth saving, but not every threat is obvious. Institutions can protect reality without understanding it fully. And anyone who learns enough about the system can try to use it — that is a permanent feature of the cosmology, not a quirk of any one being inside it. For the human question underneath the cosmic one, see Campaign → The North.

The Prime Continuum

The Prime Continuum is the total structure of existence. It encompasses all planes, realities, timelines, divine realms, and metaphysical systems. Everything that exists does so within the Prime Continuum.

The Prime Continuum is not sentient, moral, or intentional. It does not dictate outcomes, favor particular states, or distinguish between good and evil. It exists as the framework within which reality persists.

It is self-regulating. It rises and falls, threatens and stabilizes, archives and forgets — all according to its own structural pressures, on its own clocks, without needing anyone to be paying attention. The cosmology was here long before the current crisis; it will be here long after. Antagonists, however clever, are beings inside it.

The Prime Continuum requires equilibrium to remain stable. Equilibrium is not balance, symmetry, or peace. It is the dynamic tension between foundational structural forces operating within acceptable tolerances. Conflict, catastrophe, and extreme states may all exist without threatening equilibrium. Only instability beyond recovery threatens the Continuum itself.

The Prime Continuum currently maintains equilibrium through four Axioms. The interaction of those Axioms produces Continuity.

Useful distinction The Prime Continuum is the field. The four Axioms are the principles that maintain it. Continuity is what their equilibrium produces. The three concepts are different things; the rest of this page depends on keeping them straight.

Continuity

Continuity is Existence. The terms are effectively interchangeable, though "Continuity" is the more precise cosmological term. Everyday speech (and most beings inside the Continuum) call it Existence; the distinction matters only at the cosmological layer.

The misconception that matters most Existence is not an Axiom. There is no Axiom of Existence. Existence is the emergent result of the Four Axioms operating in equilibrium. Anyone — antagonist or otherwise — who talks about "the Existence axiom" is using shorthand or is mistaken. The thing that responds to strain, that gods half-sense, that the Continuum's machinery preserves, is Continuity-the-equilibrium-product, not a fifth spoke.

Continuity is the emergent result of the Four Axioms operating in equilibrium. It represents the ongoing persistence of reality itself.

Continuity is not sentient. It does not make decisions, formulate plans, or possess desires. However, it possesses function. When equilibrium is placed under sufficient strain, Continuity produces structural responses intended to preserve the persistence of existence. Monoliths are one such response.

If an Axiom is altered, fractured, replaced, or reconfigured, Continuity changes with it. Existence persists, but the nature of reality changes because the equilibrium producing it has changed. The current form of Continuity is not necessarily eternal — it is simply the form existence presently takes under the current configuration of the Four Axioms.

The Axioms

The four foundational structural principles through which the Prime Continuum currently maintains equilibrium. They emerged from necessity rather than creation and predate all known gods and civilizations.

The Axioms are sentient — but constrained entirely by their function. Each possesses a limited personality and perspective shaped by its governing principle. No Axiom can act contrary to its own nature.

Convergence Locked Most stressed

Convergence governs escalation toward extremity. Systems trend toward consolidation, centralization, and peak expression. Power accumulates, conflicts intensify, and threshold states become reachable.

Zenith Events occur as a result of Convergence driving a system toward maximum expression. Every Zenith — every cultural apex, every magical saturation, every civilizational transcendence — is Convergence doing what Convergence does.

Currently the most stressed Axiom Because Zenith Events are products of Convergence, and because each Zenith places strain on equilibrium, an environment of repeated Zenith Events accumulates strain disproportionately within Convergence — and through Convergence, on the Continuity it helps sustain. This is the Axiom currently bearing the most pressure. See The Remaking for why.
Dissolution Locked

Dissolution governs decay, fragmentation, and dispersal. Structures break down over time, concentrations disperse, and extremes become unstable. Dissolution prevents permanence and reduces accumulated intensity within systems.

Dissolution is the counterweight to Convergence. Where Convergence builds toward peak expression, Dissolution prevents that peak from becoming permanent. A reality with only Convergence would crystallize into rigid maximum states; a reality with only Dissolution would never accumulate enough to form anything in the first place.

Boundary Locked

Boundary governs separation and distinction. It maintains the differentiation between planes, identities, concepts, and structures. Boundary preserves limits and prevents uncontrolled merging or collapse into uniformity.

Boundary is why a soul is distinguishable from a body, a plane from another plane, a self from a not-self. Without Boundary, the categories that make reality navigable would not hold.

Transformation Locked

Transformation governs state change. Entities and systems transition between conditions without requiring annihilation. Transformation enables adaptation, evolution, and conversion while preventing stagnation.

Transformation is the Axiom by which a mortal becomes a god, a city becomes a ruin, a winter becomes a spring. Change without destruction. Boundary keeps things separate; Transformation lets them become something else without ceasing to be.

Axiom Fracture

An Axiom may fracture under sufficient structural strain. This is the only known mechanism by which the foundational configuration of the Prime Continuum can change without external cause. Fracture occurs in two phases.

The Shock produces immediate destabilization throughout the Prime Continuum — planar instability, magical irregularities, divine disturbances, Astral anomalies, widespread metaphysical disruptions. The Shock is sudden, but it is not the catastrophe itself. It is the announcement of one.

The Unraveling occurs over an extended period. During this phase the fractured Axiom continues to function, but increasingly imperfectly. Instability propagates from higher metaphysical layers downward through divine, planar, and eventually mortal systems. The Axiom's governing principle persists but gradually weakens until replacement, reformation, or redistribution of function occurs. The vacancy cannot persist indefinitely; the Prime Continuum will eventually establish a new equilibrium. When that occurs, Continuity changes accordingly. Existence does not end — but what it means for a thing to exist becomes different than what it meant before.

Why this matters Axiom Fracture is the door by which a being inside the Continuum could position themselves to influence — or become part of — the new equilibrium. Anyone who could engineer a Fracture and arrange to be the replacement-function for the vacated Axiom would become structural rather than merely powerful. This is the mechanism the antagonist is working toward — see The Remaking → The Dual Attack.

Paracausality

Paracausal = the reality of a thing has been changed. Not whether it exists — how it exists. The term is borrowed (Destiny); it fits because it means operating outside normal cause-and-effect. Paracausality does not mean "made magical." It is an Axiom-level concern: a thing whose place in the structure of reality has been altered.

Paracausality and the Weave are orthogonal. Paracausality describes what changed. The Weave governs why anti-magic does or doesn't suppress. Keep them separate.

Paracausality vs. the Weave

The Weave is the fabric mortals reach into to cast spells. Anti-magic severs local Weave access — it doesn't delete energy. The consequences fall out cleanly:

  • Weave-acts (spellcasting) are suppressed inside anti-magic.
  • A spell slot is innate stored energy. It persists inside anti-magic — it just can't be cast.
  • Innate, non-Weave processes (flowers' anti-magic emission, wisps' draining feed, the counter-spore plant's spore-conversion) are not suppressed. This also dissolves the "flowers cancel their own anti-magic" paradox.
Plain-language analogy for players Energy is underground water — the same for all. The Weave is the well mortals use to reach it. Anti-magic caps the well. The plant has roots — a non-Weave path to the same water — but it's a conduit with no reserves, so it needs a caster to supply a spell slot. Caster has the water; plant has the roots. Answers both "why the plant and not me?" and "why my slot?"

Counter-spore plant specification

  • Fuel with a spell slot (non-Weave conduit — works in anti-magic field).
  • Bonus action to activate.
  • The slot is the cost; slot level scales area and duration.
  • At L3, caps at 2nd-level slot (~two rungs of scaling).
  • Innate obscurity-clear — faerie-fire-like, not a cast spell.
  • Caster-only.

Forward hook: this is a category, not a one-off. Future flora-finds are a scalable non-Weave reward vein.

Paracausal entities in play

Paracausal change is rare and individual. Each known case in the campaign:

Gregory — the ability to wield magic
Autognomes are constructs. No other autognome in this world can cast. That is Gregory's paracausality and his self-surfacing tell. The resonance (Calibration, Bearing) is a consequence of the change, not the definition of it.
Mr. Blip — the control case
Personhood and soul are within normal autognome range — Blip has both. Magic is the line. Blip can't cast; Gregory can. If players wonder "is Blip like Gregory?" — no. Blip is the proof that ordinary autognomes have selves; Gregory's anomaly is the magic, not the personhood.
Wizpop surfaces it
An ordinary autognome reacting with wonder, not alarm, to Gregory casting: "You cast that — we don't — what are you?" Surfaces the difference through a peer; plants the question in Gregory's own head, gently.
The Latchling — sentience
A paracausal change of a different shape. Its reality has been altered along a different axis: it has become aware where its kind are not.

Zeniths & Monoliths

Cause and response. A Zenith is the threshold; a Monolith is what the Continuum produces when that threshold strains equilibrium past tolerance. They are two halves of one mechanism, which is why they live together.

Zenith Events

A Zenith Event occurs when a system reaches maximal intensity under the influence of Convergence. It is the full expression of a dominant force within a world, civilization, plane, species, or metaphysical system. Zenith Events are not inherently destructive. They simply represent threshold extremity. Some become disasters. Some become renewals. Some become coronations, revelations, collapses, or irreversible turnings.

Examples may include planetary elemental upheaval, divine wars, arcane singularities, civilizational transcendence, planar collapse, or stellar-scale energy harvesting.

A Zenith Event places significant strain on equilibrium. If that strain exceeds acceptable limits, Continuity may respond through the manifestation of a Monolith.

Zenith Events are natural consequences of Convergence. They occur with or without outside manipulation. Mortals seeking power often produce the most unstable Zeniths because they try to compress destiny into ritual, conquest, perfection, or control — but the Axiom would produce thresholds regardless. Zeniths happen constantly, large and small, across the Continuum at all times.

Monoliths

Monoliths are emergent manifestations of Continuity. They are not constructs, artifacts, gods, or machines. They were not designed, created, or intentionally deployed by any known intelligence.

A Monolith manifests when equilibrium strain during a Zenith Event exceeds tolerable thresholds. Upon manifestation, a Monolith anchors itself within the affected system and absorbs the dominant resonance of the Zenith Event. This resonance permanently influences the Monolith's nature and behavior.

Monoliths serve as preservation mechanisms. Their function is to preserve structures, populations, knowledge, cultures, metaphysical patterns, or other elements capable of contributing to the continued persistence of existence. Monoliths do not stop Zenith Events. They do not prevent catastrophes. They preserve continuity through catastrophe.

Once manifested, a Monolith remains as a permanent structural node within the Prime Continuum. Because Monoliths absorb resonance from the Zenith Event that created them, no two Monoliths are identical.

Critically: monoliths do not belong to anyone. They are not for anything beyond their own preservation function. The Continuum produces them whether any being observes the result or not. They were appearing long before any current faction noticed, and they will continue to appear long after.

Corrupted Monoliths

A Monolith can be corrupted by outside influence. This does not change its origin, but it distorts its function. A corrupted monolith may preserve incompletely, distort what it preserves, magnify destructive resonance, or become a vector for hostile metaphysical influence. This is especially dangerous because many factions wrongly assume monoliths are either sacred relics or evil artifacts. In truth, they are structural instruments. Mishandling them out of fear or reverence can be equally catastrophic.

The Fonains Monolith — an exceptional case

The Monolith on Fonains manifested during a Zenith Event associated with elemental upheaval, shadow corruption, and the influence of Tharizdun. The Monolith itself was not the cause of the crisis; it appeared in response to equilibrium strain generated by those events. As it manifested, it absorbed the dominant resonances present — resulting in a Monolith heavily influenced by elemental and shadow-aligned forces. Subsequent interference by Tharizdun and Vecna further corrupted its behavior and preservation processes. The Fonains Monolith is the only Monolith on record to have been destroyed.

Not representative The Fonains Monolith is an exceptional case and should not be regarded as representative of Monolith behavior as a whole. The shadow-influence, Tharizdun involvement, and the unique fact of its destruction make it an outlier, not a template. What was learned about destroying Fonains tells us about Fonains; it tells us less about what would happen to other Monoliths. Boogie's shard is a fragment of this Monolith — see World → Things in Play.

The Core

The Core Monolith is the origin point, sink, archive, and stabilizing nexus of the broader Monolith system. It is the ark of the Continuum. All Monoliths ultimately relate back to it.

The Radiant Citadel accreted around the Core over uncountable time — drawn together by the same archival pressure that produces Monoliths in the first place. The Citadel is what reached the surface of awareness; the Core itself is hidden from corporeal beings, layered beneath. Most travelers who encounter the Citadel understand only a surface truth: a place of refuge, culture, transit, and connection. Beneath that role lies its deeper function as the multiverse's great archival and stabilizing mechanism.

The Core is not public knowledge. The title Mother of the Universe belongs instead to hidden doctrine associated with the Restorers. They believe the Core can be reached, understood, and perhaps used to restore worlds, civilizations, and histories lost to catastrophe, archival failure, or metaphysical erasure. Most gods do not understand the Core. Most mortal factions do not know it exists in its true form. Even those who encounter the Radiant Citadel rarely glimpse what it sits atop.

The All-Father is the Core made sentient through accumulation, pressure, and recursive significance. Over time, as Monoliths returned resonance and meaning to the Core, a persona emerged from the function. The All-Father is real, but best understood as the personification of a cosmic function rather than a god standing outside creation. It rarely acts directly; its influence is diffuse, often felt through the Order, the Watchers, or those unusually sensitive to Continuity and Zenith pressure.

As a place and a presence The Core and All-Father here are described as cosmological function. For the Radiant Citadel as a destination and the All-Father as a presence the crew might encounter, see World. The Citadel is not near Caelestis — that prior framing is retired.

The Gods

What the gods are, in this cosmology, and the bounds of what they understand. For the specific deities the crew deals with — Beshaba, Tymora, and others — see World → Deities.

The gods did not create the Prime Continuum, the Axioms, Continuity, or the Monoliths. They exist within the system; they did not author it. Most gods understand that Monoliths appear during periods of extraordinary cosmic strain, and recognize them as indicators that equilibrium is threatened, though few understand the precise mechanism.

What the gods generally know: that Monoliths predate known divine history; that they frequently appear during world-altering crises; that destroying a Monolith does not stop the underlying event; that Monoliths preserve something, though not always in ways gods understand; that the Axioms exist, predate the gods, and that the Continuum's stability depends on them.

What very few gods understand: the relationship between Monoliths, Continuity, and Zenith Events; that Continuity and Existence are effectively the same phenomenon; that repeated Zenith Events may accumulate strain on Continuity itself.

Most gods interpret Monoliths as omens, warnings, or cosmic artifacts. In reality, they are structural responses generated by Continuity itself. Only the most ancient and knowledgeable beings have begun to suspect that repeated Zenith Events and Monolith manifestations may place strain upon the equilibrium of the Prime Continuum — Vecna among them.

The Remaking

Vecna is one of the few known entities to recognize the relationship between Zenith Events, Monoliths, and the structural equilibrium of the Prime Continuum — and the only one acting on it at scale. His discovery, his hypothesis that the strain accumulates within Continuity rather than Convergence, the figure he is drawing across Wildspace, and the dual attack on the system all live on their own pillar.
