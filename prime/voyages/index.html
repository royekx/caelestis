Voyage 001 — Orientation Complete

The Shape of a Crew

Opens in fire. The cadets are thrown into the Moonraider simulation as Captain Sardax's doomed final battle. Lesson: courage matters, but so does knowing when to retreat. Orientation circuit through Caelestis follows — administration, the cadet quarters and the bunk conflict with Vena, the Sky Dock with Kip & Pik, Stores with Mr. Blip, the Spelljammer Nexus, the gym assault course. The day closes on the Mirt errand: an assassination attempt via crate, an impossible wardrobe, Casey's first directional clue toward Viren, Tumak's theft of Mirt's Bag of Holding, and confirmation that the academy is dealing with a larger burglary problem.

What changed: the party functions like a crew in embryo. The academy theft thread is established as baseline atmosphere. Casey, Tumak, Gregory, and Boogie each leave with personal breadcrumbs.

Voyage 002 — The Day After Complete

The Shape of Looking Out

Bartholomew arrives. Tarto's brisk handoff sends him to the Weeping Goddess, where he takes the last open seat at Miken's table. The crew converges in piecemeal — Gregory first, then Boogie and Sol; the only open spot is the one Bartholomew and Miken are at, and the academy does the work of seating the sixth. Ryeback presides; Casey draws him out about hot baths and knitting; Boogie quietly suggests Miken might be a better fit than the one they were given. Miken understands, takes his bowl, leaves.

What changed: the crew becomes six. Miken is established as the cadet who wants to be folded in but isn't. Bartholomew is folded in and isn't quite sure he's welcomed yet. Caelestis begins to feel lived-in rather than scripted.

Sabotage → Convening → Realmspace Sortie
