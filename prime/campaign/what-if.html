What If

Untethered ideas. Cosmological speculation, late-arc levers, "what if a faction did X" — material that may or may not enter the world. When a What If gets developed enough to be grounded — assigned to a place, a person, a situation — it graduates to Plot Hooks. Loosely grouped by relevance. The most unstructured page in the wiki by design.

Heart-network & Wayfinding

  • What if world-hearts form a late-game transit network? If hearts are connected — emergently, structurally, like a paracausal substrate beneath ordinary navigation — then knowing the network would give access to travel between hearts. Possibly faster than spelljamming. Possibly to worlds no ordinary route reaches. Possibly uncharted worlds whose hearts are nodes even though their bodies aren't on any chart.
  • What if Toril is a doorway? Toril's heart might be mapped on the heart-network to some far-reaching world no one has heard of. The most charted Realmspace world becomes the access point for the least-charted. Through the most familiar to the most extraordinary. Kingdom Hearts shape — every charted world is a potential doorway to one that isn't.
  • What if the Wayfinder mark attunes to the body, reaches the heart? Tumak doesn't mark hearts directly — he marks celestial bodies through ritual attunement, and the mark reaches the body's heart at the cosmological layer below. The body is the address; the heart is the network. Wayfinder transit happens at heart-network depth, which is why it bypasses Forbiddance (different substrate; not Weave-based teleportation). See the resolution-pending stub at Factions → Wayfinders.
  • What if Caelestis can't be marked because it has no heart? A constructed station isn't a body in the cosmologically meaningful sense; it has no inner-truth to attune to. Forbiddance is irrelevant — there's nothing to reach. Tumak might first assume the wards block him, then realize through edge cases (a derelict ship, a small asteroid, a known dying world) that the deeper rule is "hearts attune; constructed things don't." A slow-burn cosmological reveal carried by him.
  • What if the network wants hearts as transit, not just as preparation sites? If the heart-network allows access to worlds Vecna otherwise can't reach — including worlds his figure needs to terminate at, worlds the cosmology has hidden, the Hollow itself — then hearts become routes rather than just targets. The reason the network primes hearts may not just be Zenith-prep; it may also be network-construction. Hearts become the substrate of Vecna's reach.
  • What if the Restorers want the heart-network too? If the Core (their Mother of the Universe) sits beneath the Radiant Citadel and the heart-network reaches places ordinary travel doesn't, the Restorers might be seeking the same access for entirely different reasons. A parallel agenda on the same substrate. The Order, the network, and the Restorers all reaching for the same cosmological layer through three different ideologies.

The Reach & its descendants

  • What if the Reach civilization had descendants? Some survivors escaped before the reach completed; a quiet diaspora carries cultural memory of the attempt. They might know things about Axiom-contact mechanics no one else does. Could be a faction, a single bloodline, a hidden order. Or none — the civilization died complete.
  • What if Vecna is from the Reach civilization? The deepest version of his interest in the Reach: he was there. Or he descends from it. Or he was the one who, after his god-of-secrets era, first recognized what the scattered artifacts were because he had some pre-existing connection to them. The reveal of this would be Arc III–or–later, and only if the table reaches it.
  • What if the Runekeeper's artifact is older than the Reach? Bolder reading of the Runekeeper's instrument. Not Reach-residue at all — something predating the Reach, possibly something the Reach civilization studied before they made their attempt. The Wayfinders' discipline might be older than anyone has reason to believe. Carries weight if pulled; defaults to the simpler "Reach-residue, Boundary-enforcement" reading.
  • What if other Reach artifacts surface as future MacGuffins? The set has more members than the World Surveyor. Each, when it enters play, gets its own arc. A Convergence-biased artifact that amplifies threshold events. A Dissolution-biased one that unmakes Continuity locally. A Transformation-biased one that changes a thing into another thing in violation of what should be possible. Each is a campaign arc waiting to be needed.

Alternate readers of the World Surveyor

  • What if a Watcher reads the Surveyor? A Watcher's cosmological understanding sits between the crew's and Vecna's. They might read at the operational depth — see the survey patterns the network sees — without quite seeing what Vecna sees. A Watcher who got their hands on it would extract more than the crew but less than the antagonist.
  • What if a Restorer reads it? The Restorers' doctrine is parallel to Vecna's understanding — they see structure, they look for the Core. A Restorer might read at the structural depth and see what Vecna sees. The implication: a Restorer holding the device is essentially as dangerous as the network holding it, just pointed in a different direction.
  • What if a god reads it? Gods see less than they think they do (see Cosmology → The Gods). A god might read at surface only — they see the same world's-hidden-geography the crew sees, and may be surprised by their own limitation. Or — bolder — a god who has begun to see past their portfolio might read deeper. Beshaba reading Sol's world through the Surveyor is a possible late-arc moment.

Crew disposition of the Surveyor

  • What if the crew destroys the World Surveyor? If they hold it and decide it's too dangerous, they might break the housing and bury the heart. The heart survives — it's paracausal, Reach-residue, possibly unbreakable. They'd be hiding it, not eliminating it. Some future finder would learn what they buried.
  • What if they give it to a third party? The Watchers, a Restorer they don't know is one, a friendly faction they trust. Most third parties are not equipped to hold it well. The Order would probably take it and lock it away again, restarting the cycle. A Restorer would use it — for different purposes than Vecna, but it would still be used. There may be no "safe" custodian.
  • What if they bring it to Tumak's homeworld? If the Runekeeper's artifact and the Surveyor's heart are both Reach-residue, both Boundary-biased, putting them in the same room might do something. Resonance. Amplification. Recognition between fragments. This is a card to play very carefully — but it's a card.

The network and the PCs

  • What if the network notices Tumak? If they learn that Tumak has Wayfinder abilities — and they're hunting for ways to reach hearts beyond what the artifacts give them — they might follow him, try to exploit him, or try to acquire him. Card to play carefully. The healthy version: they file him as an anomaly, investigate further only if circumstances warrant. The unhealthy version: he becomes a quest object, which turns him from PC to plot device. Don't go there unless the table earns it.
  • What if the network learns about the Latchling? A paracausal anomaly with sentience could be of interest to them — possibly as a sign that Gregory's resonance is producing usable phenomena. They might target the Sea Dock specifically, or try to recruit Rindle, or try to acquire the Latchling itself.
  • What if a captured network operative reveals more than they should? If a State B1 graduation outcome ends with a live saboteur, what they know becomes intelligence. How much of the plan do they understand? Almost certainly less than Vecna — operatives know their cell. But what they know about routing protocols, recognition codes, contact rituals, could let the academy or the crew unwind a chunk of the surveillance operation.
