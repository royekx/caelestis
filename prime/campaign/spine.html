The North

The campaign's central question. The thing the whole story is, underneath the cosmology:

Who decides what's real?

Every faction has an answer (see Factions & Characters). Every PC's arc is a personal version of the same question. Vecna's answer — I decide, and everyone else's reality is a footnote — is what makes him the antagonist. The crew's answer is what makes them the crew.

The Weather of Reality

The cosmological climate of this campaign. Three things are happening at once across the cosmos, and they pressurize everything the crew encounters:

  • Continuity is under strain. Vecna's project is bending the system. Paracausal events are more common than they should be; gods reach further than their portfolios usually allow; cosmic powers stretch.
  • Astra is expanding. A wounded paracausal celestial is widening its reach through Starchild tethers. Casey is one. The cosmic pull is real and not Vecna-aligned — a second cosmic pressure operating in parallel.
  • The Restorers are moving. A third hidden agenda, unknown to most institutions, reaching toward the same cosmological pressure points the Watchers monitor — for different reasons.

The crew won't see most of this directly for a long time. What they'll see is the weather — strange events that don't quite explain themselves, NPCs hinting at things they shouldn't know, recurring patterns that suggest a coherent force behind them. The job is to let the weather be felt without explaining it.

Bartholomew Grayson

Bartholomew Grayson

 The Almosts — a missing shape he's unconsciously circling. Recognition arc, not search.
Arc shape

Bartholomew's arc centers on the Almosts: a missing wildshape form he's unconsciously circling, something already present he has been avoiding. The accelerant is an unbloomed flower from the tyrant ship's garden — see Factions → Unaffiliated → The unbloomed bud.

Boogie

Boogie
Plasmoid Divine Soul Sorcerer
 Container for archival residue. The Fonains shard sits in his chest.
Arc shape

Boogie carries the Fonains shard — a fragment of the only monolith ever destroyed, Tharizdun-corrupted. He's becoming a container for archival residue: shard, half-ship, salvage logbook. The "urge to dance" is the Pulse triggered by the corrupted shard.

RP-only mechanic. Don't surface the Pulse mechanically; let it shape his behavior.

Where it's moving: the shard is the proof-of-concept for removing a point from Vecna's figure — late-arc weight. Boogie's arc is about whether he survives that pressure intact, and whether he stays himself as the residue accumulates.

Arc shape

Casey is a Starchild — his tether extends Astra's reach. Polly is his patron, the familiar voice. She is Astra's proxy, bonded to Casey's brother. The connection has not been named openly in fiction; narrative refers to her as "a familiar presence" or "a familiar voice."

Gregory

Gregory
Autognome Abjurer Wizard
 Paracausal — an autognome who can cast. The Existence Axiom's tuning fork.
Arc shape

Gregory is paracausal: no other autognome in this world can cast. The resonance he feels (Calibration, Bearing) is a consequence of the change, not the definition. He is a tuning fork for the Existence Axiom — anomalies surface near him.

Active hook: the Latchling at the Sea Dock, his first anomaly. The arc is the journey (Rindle as obstacle), not the destination (he knows where).

Where it's moving: more anomalies, surfaced through his resonance. Each one further reframes his own origin — what does it mean that a constructed being can do this? Reaches toward the cosmological question his existence poses.

Sol Fortuna

Sol Fortuna
Astral Elf Paladin of Beshaba
 The Misfortuned. Survivor of Aethris's completed Zenith. Pursued by the CRC.
Arc shape

Sol was born "Misfortuned" on Aethris — a Tymora-devoted world that completed its Zenith. He survived a failed ritual, was recruited by a Watcher, and now manifests Beshaba's Oath of Misfortune. Beshaba speaks minimally: "Stand," "Again," "Continue."

Tumak Swan

Arc shape

Twin halflings disguised as one person, both partial Wayfinders. Homeworld hides itself cosmically; the current Lunarfoot Runekeeper is dimming, which is why both twins were sent — a hedge that creates its own Zenith risk if both return together.

The Acts

Four acts. Detail decreases as the acts move further from current play — Act 1 is concrete, Act 4 is directional. Each act is the campaign's shape at that scale; specific session prep lives in Voyages.

Act 1 — Spelljammer Academy

The tutorial. Cadets going from raw recruits to Driftwardens. Surface text: training, missions, the academy's politics. Underneath: the cosmology asserting itself, the network running cover, the figure being drawn. The crew doesn't know yet, and that's the point.

Goal & end point

Graduation. The crew becomes Driftwardens, kept together as a unit. The Brig heist Fork resolves: State A (thief escapes with the World Surveyor → Arc II is chase) / State B1 (crew returns it to the academy → contest with institutional backing) / State B2 (crew keeps it → literacy decode at their pace).

Where we are

Module 3 of the published Spelljammer Academy. Pacing extended for breathing room and to let the sabotage mystery stir. Current crew status: between voyages — last session ended on the tyrant ship outside Toril's orbit. Voyage 004 is Fire in the Galley + Behold H'Catha. See Voyages for current beat.

The shape

Three published modules become the tutorial: Orientation (V01–V02), Trial by Fire / Tyrant Ship (V03), Behold H'Catha (V04). Then graduation. Each module is the surface; underneath, every "ordinary" incident (the thefts, the neogi, the sabotage, the missing ships, the clockwork horrors, the H'Catha meteor) is a component seeded so the crew can assemble the pattern themselves later.

The Vocath grudge-op is the cover. The network's reconnaissance against the Brig is the real operation, running for months underneath. The embedded saboteur is already on station, having arrived through legitimate channels. Graduation chaos is the cover for the breach.

Critical beats

  • Voyage 004 cold open: Fire in the Galley. Ryeback dies; Miken's betrayal lands hard.
  • Behold H'Catha: the final published module. Recover the adamantine meteor. Tyrant ship disguise to reach H'Catha. Sol's first CRC encounter lands in this window as an objective-based escape, not a fight.
  • Graduation: crew becomes Driftwardens, kept together. The meteor is handed over and reclassified above their clearance — the breadcrumb that retroactively loads the whole act. The Fork resolves.

Active hooks in Act 1

  • The H'Catha Meteor — forthcoming
  • The Sabotage Sigil — visible, traceable
  • The Missing Ships — escalating
  • The Latchling — active (Gregory's arc)
  • The World Surveyor at graduation — imminent
  • Sol / Aethris & Beshaba — active (CRC incoming)
Act 2 — Driftwardens in the Field

Missions. The crew earns rank, learns the Fleet's politics, runs operations in Wildspace, and starts encountering the network's traces in the field. The cosmological awareness builds from local strange events into a coherent pattern.

Goal & end point

The crew recognizes the network as a coherent enemy before any institution does. The pattern engine resolves: the sigil, the missing ships, the resonance-cores, the heart-readings (if they hold the World Surveyor) all converge into one operation by one being for one purpose. Arc 2 ends with that recognition and the first attempt to act on it institutionally.

The shape

Mission-based. The handler (TBD) issues contracts; the crew runs them; each mission seeds a piece of the larger picture. The Fork state from graduation determines tempo — chase, contest, or literacy decode.

The world-hearts chase runs as a directional pattern through Arc 2: the crew (if holding the Surveyor) and the network reach for the same physical sites for different reasons. Sometimes the crew arrives first; sometimes the site is already touched. The realization that something is racing them is a slow-burn beat of Arc 2.

Open development

Arc 2 needs more work. The handler is unnamed; the mission rhythm isn't yet locked; the specific worlds that get visited are TBD; the network's field operations need specifying per location. This is the chat that will design Arc 2 when we sit down to it.

Active hooks (planted, ready to surface in Arc 2)

  • Boogie / The Fonains Shard
  • Casey / Virenspace & Zerathis
  • Tumak / The Becoming
Act 3 — The Recognition

The crew acts on what they've learned. They know the network exists; they know what it's doing; they start trying to stop or redirect it. Institutional friction builds — the Order, the Fleet, the Restorers, and the network all have stakes. The campaign opens up.

Direction

Less detail. Act 3's shape depends on Act 2's outcomes. Possibilities the spine carries forward:

  • The Restorers surface as a third force.
  • The Runekeepers become legible; Tumak's homeworld arc moves toward resolution.
  • The crew encounters a second Reach artifact, hostile or not.
  • The world-hearts as a transit substrate becomes operationally relevant (see What If).
  • The Hollow becomes a real destination, possibly reachable.

Open. To be developed as Act 2 reveals what the campaign actually wants Act 3 to be.

Act 4 — The Cast

The inscription completes or fails. Vecna's spell gets its one cast. The crew has done what they could; the cosmology resolves around the result.

Direction

Minimal at this stage. Act 4 will be shaped by everything the crew has done in Acts 2 and 3. The shape of success for the campaign is not "stop Vecna" but "change what success would mean for him." The crew bends his casting precision, his options, his timing, his confidence in the outcome — to the point where the cast misses, partially succeeds, or succeeds at a cost he didn't intend.

For the failure-state shape (what happens if the crew loses), see Cosmology → What Vecna wants from the Reach. The Convergence rule says he gets one cast.
