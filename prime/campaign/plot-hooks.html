Campaign·Active Threads

Plot Hooks

Threads that live in the world right now. Each has a foundation in Cosmology, Realms, or Factions & Characters — pull on any of them and they're ready to develop. For untethered ideas and cosmological brainstorm, see What If. For antagonist throughline specifically, see The Remaking.

Running the Hooks

Each hook is tagged by type — character / place / event / object — and many hooks carry more than one. The tags tell you how the crew might encounter the hook: through a person, a location, a happening, or a thing in hand. Use them to scan for what fits the current voyage or the current PC focus.

The give-and-take rule A hook's "if pulled" is a direction, not a script. If players pull it, that direction becomes real and gets developed. If they pull it somewhere unexpected, bend the destination — but keep the route back to the spine. No hook is wasted; unpulled hooks stay planted for later. Live status lives in the Campaign Tracker; here are the durable shapes.
Tag legend Character — encountered through a person. Place — encountered by going somewhere. Event — encountered by an incident happening. Object — encountered through a thing in hand.

Active Threads

The H'Catha Meteor Object Event Forthcoming
What it is
Adamantine that struck the Spire on H'Catha. Forthcoming fetch quest — the crew approaches via tyrant ship disguise in Voyage 004 (Foundling can't survive H'Catha's beholder patrols). Already logged as threshold-resonant in the Fleet archive (FIA-007).
If pulled
Tier 1 — it will hum on the same frequency as the resonance-cores, Gregory's core, Boogie's shard when retrieved. Tier 2 (held) — it is a fragment of an Ortholith, shed or aimed. Tier 3 (reserve, never decide early) — the strike was bait.
Routes to spine
The first physical piece of the cosmology to pass through the crew's hands. Confirms in hindsight that Arc I was never mundane. The graduation hand-off triggers the reclassification beat.
The Abandoned Beholders Character Place
What it is
The tyrant ship had beholder crew. They left. Why is unknown — intentionally.
If pulled
Whatever a player latches onto becomes the development. Beholders are revered/iconic; the gravity is built in. Could lead to a beholder world, a fled colony, a thing they fled from.
Routes to spine
If they fled, what made beholders abandon a ship? Pressure. Something Continuum-scale moving through H'cathan space. The "why" can become a Zenith precursor whenever needed.
The Clockwork Horrors & Resonance-Cores Character Object
What it is
Made things consuming made things, cores of resonance-attuned crystal — same supply line behind other incidents (the Vocath thread in the FIA).
If pulled
The destructive face of Continuity under stress; a supply chain leads back toward a manufacturer, which leads toward the network.
Routes to spine
They are Continuity-as-erasure given form. The cores tie them to the same circuit as everything else Gregory resonates with.
The Sabotage Sigil / Neogi Brand Object Event
What it is
The same mark on the sabotage plates and beneath the neogi's chitin. One hand behind two incidents.
If pulled
The crew starts tracking a symbol Fleet Intelligence can't match — the eye-in-a-wheel (subtle Vecna helm). Leads toward recognizing a coherent enemy before any institution does.
Routes to spine
This is the literal thread that converts "isolated incidents" into "a pattern" — the core epistemic move of the whole campaign.
The Missing Ships (incl. Saerthe's) Event Object
What it is
Thefts escalated from items → vessels. Saerthe's own miniature is gone; the model matches the half-ship inside Boogie.
If pulled
Where do stolen ships go, and why ships specifically? Toward the Syndicate (profits from instability) or the network (needs transport for relics/agents).
Routes to spine
"Thefts of charts, records, navigational knowledge" is a named campaign-facing symptom of Vecna's method. The escalation to ships means the operation is scaling.
The Latchling Place Object Character Bearing given V003
What it is
First anomaly Gregory resonates with. At the Sea Dock, near cargo. Rindle Gearloft protects it. Gregory's repair work may have been the local condition that let the ripple resolve into it.
If pulled
The arc is the journey (Rindle as obstacle), not the destination (Gregory knows where). Two-way recognition when they meet. Reframes Gregory's own origin.
Routes to spine
Proof-of-concept that Gregory's resonance is real and that the Axiom is producing anomalies near him. First rung of his tuning-fork function.
Boogie / The Fonains Shard Character Object FIA match exists
What it is
The stone inside Boogie matches the Fonains monolith fragment in the archive — a piece of the only monolith ever destroyed. He also now carries a half-ship and a salvage logbook inside his chest. He is becoming a container for archival residue.
If pulled
Boogie is a walking corrupted-archive question. What does it mean that fragments collect in him? Is he becoming a monolith-analogue?
Routes to spine
Destroyed monoliths = catastrophic archival failure. Boogie may be where some of that lost archive went. Also load-bearing for late-arc: the shard is the only recorded instance of a monolith being destroyed.
Casey / Virenspace & Zerathis Character Place
What it is
The wardrobe vision pointed past Hakatha to the Viren system; Zerathis the spellcaster gifted it. Patron Polly guides him (the familiar presence; bonded to his brother).
If pulled
A directional arc toward Virenspace — a whole system to develop when the table is ready. Polly's nature is the slow reveal.
Routes to spine
Whether Viren carries Watcher / Zenith / monolith pressure is an open development area — wire it in when Casey leans that way.
Sol / Aethris & Beshaba Character Place Beshaba heard
What it is
Aethris is logged as a manufactured Zenith with the Prism Ascendant still standing — "one survivor escaped." Sol is the survivor. He has now heard Beshaba and manifests black lightning (Oath of Misfortune). The Crystalline Retrieval Construct hunts him.
If pulled
Sol's homeworld is a completed Vecna operation. His existence is the loose end of a Zenith that "succeeded." His home actively pursues him.
Routes to spine
Sol is living proof of what other worlds are being aimed toward. He is the campaign's "this already happened once" — and a divine has a stake in him persisting against erasure. Continuity at personal scale.
Tumak / The Becoming Character Place
What it is
Twin halflings (Wan & Tu) as one person. Both partial Wayfinders. Homeworld hides itself cosmically; the Lunarfoot Runekeeper is dimming; simultaneous twin return is a Zenith risk. Runes surface on skin near cosmic threats.
If pulled
A homeworld that defends itself by being unfindable — and a return that could trip a threshold. The runes are a built-in proximity detector for cosmic danger. If the Runekeeper fails entirely, the world surfaces, which is itself a Zenith event.
Routes to spine
Tumak is a Zenith waiting to be forced — by his own homecoming. Convergence + Intensification, personal scale. Also a window onto the Runekeepers as a hidden order with a different relationship to the cosmology than any public faction.
The World Surveyor at graduation Object Event Place
What it is
The World Surveyor is held in the Brig. The network's embedded saboteur will breach during graduation chaos. Three resolution outcomes — see The Remaking → The Fork.
If pulled
Crew engagement with the heist directly. The chase, the literacy decode, the institutional aftermath — all flow from how this resolves.
Routes to spine
This is the spine for Arc I → II. The artifact is what the antagonist is reaching for; the Brig is where it lives; the heist is when it moves. Every outcome routes forward.
