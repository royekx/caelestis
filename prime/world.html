Caelestis

The station. Home base. The campaign's broader headquarters and long-term operational base. A strategic platform and a symbolic one: the place where military readiness, exploration, cosmological mystery, and interworld obligation meet.

Lore Bible correction The Citadel is not close to Caelestis. The prior framing has been retired — see Continuum → The Core & the Citadel. Caelestis is a strategic node on its own merits, not a Citadel satellite.

Structure — one station, two functions

Caelestis is a single station containing two distinct operational wings:

The Academy wing

Mirt's domain. Training, cadets, simulation deck, classrooms, the Spelljammer Nexus, the Weeping Goddess refectory. Where the crew came up. The cadet life ends when the crew graduates.

The Fleet operations wing

The handler's domain. The contract board, the working docks, visiting captains. Part base, part market. The space-bar-adjacent social layer lives here. Graduation = crossing the station from school side to operations side. The threshold is internal — not relocating, just earning the doors.

Realmspace

Toril's Wildspace. The crew's current operating area (the geographic leash — see Campaign → Scope). The published canonical material for this sphere stands as backdrop; this campaign uses it as a junction more than a destination — see the Crossroads Principle.

Travel within Realmspace is fast — days between worlds via spelljamming helm. Between Realmspace and the rest of the Continuum is the phlogiston, which is reserved for senior-crew long-haul work (the rank-buys-range progression).

The Anchor Worlds

Two reachable worlds that show the crew their own future. Together they are the two faces of the warning — and why the crew fights.

Aethris — a completed manufactured Zenith Cosmic anchor

Sol's homeworld. A world that reached its own Zenith — at a moment the network encouraged, because the responding monolith lands exactly where Vecna's figure needs a mark. The Prism Ascendant is the architecture Aethris's society built for the Zenith itself (a Tymora-worship civilization compressing destiny into ritual). Vecna's role was selection and timing, not invention. Aethris did the hard work of approaching the threshold on its own; he made sure it crossed at the right moment. The monolith that responded now stands inside his figure. Sol is the one survivor, the loose end.

Standing here is standing inside what other worlds are being aimed toward. This is someone else deciding what's real, made permanent at a planetary scale.

The Misfortuned
The category Sol was born into on Aethris — those whom the world's dominant Tymora-worship considered aberrations. A failed Zenith ritual was the trigger of his escape; his survival is the world's loose end.
The Fortuned
Sol's counterparts. The fortune-blessed Aethris elite. They commissioned the Crystalline Retrieval Construct after Sol's escape, to bring him back.
The Prism Ascendant
The architecture of the Zenith. Still standing. Function unclear at this scale; one of the things Aethris-as-destination will eventually reveal.

The Ascension World Specifics pending Cosmic anchor

A reachable world where Vecna once forced a threshold and won, locally — a small, personal ascension (not the canonical Sigil event) that left the world a scar: where "who decides what's real" was decided permanently in one being's favor and everything else became a footnote to him.

Standing on it is standing inside the failure state — a local Eve of Ruin you can walk. This is Vecna deciding. Together with Aethris, it's the campaign's thesis made into ground the crew can stand on.

Tumak's homeworld Unnamed by design

Hides itself cosmically. The world is a living thing that decides what reaches it and what does not. Tumak does not know its name — this is intentional and cultural, not a gap. The name is withheld from the exiled child to preserve the integrity of the journey. Tumak must identify home through remembered sensory fragments and cultural impressions rather than by name or direct coordinates.

The world's concealment is maintained by the Runekeeper, whose body bears the inscription. The current Lunarfoot Runekeeper is dimming, which is why Wan and Tu were both sent as Wayfinders — a hedge against catastrophe that creates its own Zenith risk if both return together. Convergence + Intensification at personal scale.

Full mechanical detail in the Wayfinder/Runekeeper mechanics doc. See also: the Tumak / The Becoming seed.

Cairn Station Open thread

A station the artifact's trail eventually leads to. Sits in the territory of an astral dreadnought — the apex predator of the Astral Sea, a titanic one-eyed chain-tailed leviathan that hunts the dead matter of the cosmos.

The dreadnought is the lock, not the boss

The crew does not fight the dreadnought. It's the environmental pressure that makes Cairn Station a white-knuckle destination — you avoid it, slip past it, time your movement to its patrol, get in and out before the eye turns your way.

Why information is hidden there
If you want to stash a piece of a cosmos-ending working where no institution will stumble on it, you put it in a dreadnought's hunting ground. The monster is the lock. Nobody audits a place that eats auditors. "Cairn" reads right — a cairn is a marker over the dead, and this is a station perched in a boneyard.
Why it's thematically loaded
An astral dreadnought eats the dead matter of the cosmos — it's the Astral's mechanism for unmaking what's already ended. A god-corpse-eater squatting over the network's secret is the charter's question wearing teeth: who decides what's real? The thing that eats what's already been decided-against.
Future-seed (don't decide yet)
The dreadnought's presence at Cairn may not be coincidence. Something about the network's working may attract these creatures, or the network may use the territory deliberately as a vault. Don't decide now; the resonance is there if needed.

Open question — what is Cairn Station, in character?

Decide when approached Two clean directions:
  • A) Derelict / dead site. A literal cairn — an abandoned archive or wreck in the boneyard. The network goes there to retrieve the next chart-piece from a place no one guards because the dreadnought does it for free. Heist-against-environment.
  • B) Inhabited. A hidden outpost of fences/scholars, existing in the dreadnought's shadow precisely because that keeps the authorities away. The network has to deal with them to get the next piece. Gives you a recurring location and NPCs.

Caelestis · Academy & Fleet

Mirt Headmaster

Academy headmaster. The cadets' patron and the school's institutional shield. Has many enemies — being headmaster is what's keeping him standing. Vocath's whole operation is aimed at prising him loose from that shield.

Mirt knows more than he is saying. The wardrobe is his. The Viren reference is his. Not yet ready to discuss it; the Convening will surface it. Open thread: Mirt's reputation outcome at graduation — saved (heroic), genuinely suffers (cloud into Arc II), or ambiguous. Pending

Boatswain Tarto

Clipped, direct, unsentimental. Quietly approving when earned. Does not perform authority. Runs the simulation deck, assigns cadets, co-runs the Trial by Fire briefing. "Wilder or not, you're no different than any other crew member once you're aboard."

Saerthe Abyzine

Silver-eyed, measured, sees too much. Stern gaze that shifts to a wide grin. Lets fragments slip, never full pictures. Co-runs the Trial by Fire briefing. One of his ships has gone missing — the thefts have escalated from items to vessels and Saerthe is openly connecting the loss to the larger pattern.

Potentially the figure who keeps the crew together post-graduation (mentor framing). Pending

Mr. Blip

Stores. Helpful, odd, pragmatic, a bit too informed about supply weirdness. Doesn't volunteer what he knows. Not complicit in the dock-seam theft — the leak is between his inventory and the Sky Dock handlers. Reward and logistics vector.

Blip is the control case for Gregory's paracausality — see Continuum → Paracausal entities. Personhood and soul within normal autognome range; magic is the line.

Petty Officer Winston Ryeback

Giff galley cook at the Weeping Goddess. Brash, raucous, affectionate. Bear-hug energy. Hears everything. Belongs to no crew. "Eat. All of you."

Sign: Feast of the Moon (Sagittarius, holiday). Stars {5, 7, 8}. "Walks unseen; between worlds." Casey's first canonical Stellar Reading hit. His custom pistol was stolen in the Academy thefts — recurring grumble. Use him for warmth and texture.

Sorcur

Thri-kreen receptionist at administration. Voice enters minds rather than ears. Hands out packets, vouchers, and temporary access stars.

Kip & Pik

Two gnomes who speak almost as one mind. Sky Dock. Efficient, mirrored, practical, faintly frazzled. Run ship-anatomy drills with cadets. Drop the line "Some crews move people, not freight" casually — the mission-type breadcrumb.

Rindle Gearloft

Sea Dock worker. Rock gnome — dark hair tied back, goggles pushed up, vest crowded with tools. Found the Latchling. Hiding it. Protective, not malicious. Reads as guarded and over-specific if approached. Gregory's social obstacle on the Latchling arc.

Other cadets & allies

Miken Haverstance Live thread

Cadet. Eager, slightly awkward, easy to like. Wants social footing; afraid of letting his crew down. Bartholomew's first table-mate at the Weeping Goddess.

One small, late, leveraged asset of Vocath's network — blackmailed via a young fiancé (debt/danger a cadet's stipend can't fix). Sympathetic, arguable. Wizpop Active in Voyage 003

An ordinary autognome surviving aboard the tyrant ship. Reacts with wonder, not alarm to Gregory casting: "You cast that — we don't — what are you?" Surfaces Gregory's paracausality through a peer; plants the question in his own head, gently.

Noncombatant. The clockwork scavengers killed Wizpop's autognome colleagues; Wizpop is the last survivor.

Ezra Crossroads

Watcher-in-training. The Foundling's errand is to collect him for a rendezvous and in-field training at H'Catha; he attaches to the crew for that mission. A known quantity to the Fleet, riding along to learn the job — not a cipher.

Antagonists & threats

Vocath Plan locked

A mercane (interstellar merchant). His grudge against Mirt is commercial and personal, through the school. He doesn't need to kill Mirt or destroy the school — he needs to disgrace Mirt enough to prise him loose from his protection so someone else finishes him.

The thefts and sabotage are manufactured noise — cover for the chart heist running quiet underneath, and a campaign to wreck Mirt's standing. Vocath happily owns the grudge because it's perfect cover. Not a cultist. The network is the hidden hand using his operation. Full detail at Campaign → The Network.

Qitru

Vocath's intermediary (or her proxy). The voice on the other end of Miken's hushed magical contact. "You had an agreement. You have to do this."

In the module, Vocath's people hunt Miken to retrieve him. Reframed: the network wants Miken silenced — he's a burned, one-use asset who can identify the intermediary/disc. Qitru's job is cleanup. Future hook.

The Crystalline Retrieval Construct (CRC) Sol's antagonist

Built by the Fortuned on Aethris after Sol's escape — his escape was the trigger, not a predicted contingency. Mission: capture, correction, return. Not murder.

Signature line: "Sol Fortuna. You are required."

Tone: cold cosmic inevitability, sterile certainty, beautiful-yet-wrong, no taunts or posturing. Inspirations: T-1000, Amazo, Cell, cosmic construct. First encounter on Caelestis is an objective-based escape (not a kill). Recurring nemesis; thematic foil to Sol's question "Am I a person or a function?"

Open: who on Aethris cared enough to commission it, and why. Pending

The unbloomed bud / "the Almosts"

Bartholomew's arc accelerant. An unbloomed flower from the tyrant ship's garden. Stirs the Almosts — an inkling and a direction, not a revelation. Blooms via narrative milestones of his becoming + possibly its provenance location. Accelerant, not key — a dead flower never stalls the arc.

The Almosts: a missing shape Bartholomew is unconsciously circling, something already present he has been avoiding. Recognition, not search. Creature design held until DM observes player in actual play. Key observational flags: how he uses Beast Sense/Beast Bond, treatment of the star map, whether he reaches for Starry Form or Wild Shape under pressure.

Cosmic / patron figures

Polly Casey's patron

The familiar voice that reaches Casey. Heard in Mirt's wardrobe in V01 ("Go there") and at the Sky Dock rail in V02 as an unnamed presence. Casey recognizes her without seeing her.

Polly is Astra's proxy — bonded to Casey's brother. The connection has not been named openly in fiction; narrative refers to her as "a familiar presence" or "a familiar voice."

Astra is a sentient paracausal celestial body wounded by the Existence Axiom, expanding via Starchild tethers. Polly is the voice; Astra is the source. "Starchildren" is the locked proper term for people like Casey. Casey's tethers extend Astra's reach and change the fundamental nature of those regions.

See Campaign → The Weather of Reality for Astra's widening reach as a non-Vecna cosmic pull.

Zerathis Prior Starchild

The spellcaster who gifted the wardrobe to Mirt. A prior Starchild, now consumed into the paracausal layer of Virenspace. Casey's predecessor along this path. Open question: did Zerathis recruit other Starchildren — determining if Virenspace is a two- or three-plus overlap site.

Beshaba Sol's patron

Goddess of misfortune. Now heard by Sol; Oath of Misfortune is live; black lightning manifests. Beshaba speaks minimally to him: "Stand," "Again," "Continue."

Tone calibration: she does not deliver speeches. Her presence is the minimum word needed to keep him standing against erasure. Sol's thematic role is existence through endurance; Beshaba is the divine bet on him persisting.

Things in Play

Named objects that matter. Concrete things the crew has touched, holds, or is moving toward.

The Foundling The crew's ship

A hammerhead-class spelljammer. The crew's vessel for the Realmspace sortie onward. Cannot survive the H'Catha approach (beholder patrols) — disguise logic puts the crew on the tyrant ship instead. The Foundling waits at the system's edge, positioned to detour and double back. Its errand: collect Ezra, the Watcher-in-training.

Boogie's shard / The Fonains Monolith Already in play

The stone inside Boogie's nucleus matches the Fonains monolith fragment in the FIA archive — a piece of the only monolith ever destroyed. Tharizdun-corrupted. A corrupted Continuity shard embedded in a living plasmoid.

Quietly one of the most important objects in the entire campaign. The shard's existence is the proof-of-concept for removing a point from the figure — the late-game answer to "how do you stop a finished spell," sitting dormant in a PC's chest, sourced from another table's history. See Campaign → Back-burner.

He also carries a half-ship (V01) and a salvage logbook inside his chest. He is becoming a container for archival residue. His "urge to dance" is a Pulse triggered by the corrupted shard.

The H'Catha Meteor Threshold-resonant

Adamantine that struck the Spire on H'Catha. Retrieved as a fetch quest in Voyage 004. Already logged as threshold-resonant in the Fleet archive (FIA-007). Hums on the same frequency as Boogie's shard, Gregory's core, and the clockwork resonance-cores.

Held at three depths: surface (a meteor) · middle (a fragment of an Ortholith, shed or aimed) · deep (the strike was bait). Decide depth as the crew earns it. The crew handing it over at graduation triggers the reclassification beat — the breadcrumb that retroactively loads Arc I.

The artifact (the network's prize) Specifics pending

An artifact no one at Caelestis understood. Miscatalogued, shelved as a curio, lifted quietly by the network's saboteur via the bribed dock seam under cover of Vocath's grudge-operation. Points the network toward the next point on Vecna's figure.

Candidates (all fit "miscatalogued curio that finds or touches monoliths"): locator-lens, monolith-key, monolith-fragment. Lean: locator-lens for the no-railroad-rule safety. Full discussion at Campaign → The Artifact.

The Latchling Sea Dock

The first anomaly Gregory's resonance locates. A paracausal entity — its change is sentience where its kind has none. Hidden at the Sea Dock by Rindle Gearloft, who is protecting it from the academy. Gregory's repair work on a latch may have been the local condition that let the ripple resolve into it.

The arc is the journey (obstacles) more than the destination (Gregory knows where). Two-way recognition when they meet. Reframes Gregory's own origin.

The transmutation sigil / eye-in-wheel mark

The mark on the sabotage plates and beneath the neogi's chitin. The visual rhyme that converts isolated incidents into a pattern. A subtle Vecna helm. Fleet Intelligence can't match it — the crew tracks it before any institution does. The pattern-engine seed.

The clockwork horrors & resonance-cores

Made things consuming made things to make more of themselves, with no concept that the consumed were ever someone. The destructive face of the Existence Axiom. Cores of resonance-attuned crystal — same supply line behind other incidents.

They are the exact inverse of Gregory's question. Where Gregory asks "is a made thing a person?", the horrors are reality answering no, it's inventory.

The disc-marked crate cache

At the Sky Dock. Where Vocath's grudge-loot pools — the visible thefts (Ryeback's pistol, restricted supplies, navigational charts). Cover for the quiet artifact-lift underneath. The crew found it and didn't open it.
