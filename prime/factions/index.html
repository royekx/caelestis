Order of the All-Father

An interworld organization that interprets the influence of the All-Father and acts to preserve Continuity where possible. Recruits from many worlds and cultures; members include mystics, scholars, spelljammers, operatives, diplomats, ritual specialists, and planetary wardens. Operates with two field arms: the Watchers (sanctioned) and Rogue Watchers (heterodox).

Watchers

The Order's sanctioned field arm. A known partner organization to the Fleet. Authority is divine-by-belief: members hold that the All-Father charged them, and carry themselves with the bearing of a priesthood.

Function: observe, report on, and where necessary intervene in worlds approaching dangerous cosmological thresholds. Their mandate is preservation of Continuity, not universal rescue — a distinction the order takes seriously.

Operations rely on the Fleet for transport and reach; the Fleet relies on the order for moral mandate and divine cover.

Rogue Watchers

Watchers who do not follow the Order's protocols as closely as it would prefer. They operate on their own judgment — sometimes intervening where doctrine would not, sometimes failing to intervene where doctrine would expect, sometimes taking the work in a different direction than they were sent. A range, not a category. Some are nearly indistinguishable from sanctioned Watchers; some are openly heterodox.

The Order tolerates them with varying degrees of patience depending on the individual and the era. A Rogue Watcher who saves a world by acting outside doctrine is quietly celebrated; one who violates a precept and creates downstream harm is censured. The line between principled disobedience and dangerous overreach is not always clear to either side.

They appear in the world as local protectors, unorthodox interventionists, or quietly excommunicated former colleagues of sanctioned Watchers. Most worlds that encounter one cannot distinguish a Watcher from a Rogue Watcher.

Caelestis Academy & Fleet

A self-made, non-governmental organization headquartered at the Caelestis station in Realmspace. The Academy is the recruitment and training arm; the Fleet is the operational body that grew from it. Authority is self-bestowed by reputation rather than granted by any council of worlds.

Operations are for-hire and frequently covert. Most worlds do not know that Wildspace exists; the Fleet blends in, deals with the few locals who are read in, and leaves without rewriting a civilization's reality. Contracts are load-bearing — they are how the Fleet sustains itself.

Cadet graduates are called Driftwardens — the in-world term for the operational corps.

The Bridge

The Fleet's senior command. Members oversee policy, training, funding, logistics, security, and mission assignment. Not omniscient; not always united. Individual officers vary in how much they understand about cosmological stakes, and in how they read any given incident.

Spectrum

Mid-tier ideological and operational factions across the cosmos.

Concordance

An ideological force concerned with legitimacy, coherence, and metaphysical orthodoxy. Members work to define which version of reality is acceptable and to enforce that definition. Appear in the world as scholars, adjudicators, ritual authorities, doctrinal reformers, and institutional purists.

Fracture Cult

Exists to create peak events. Members hold that rupture, catastrophe, concentration of will, and symbolic climax can break stagnant reality open and reveal higher truth, power, or destiny. Accelerate worlds toward Zenith conditions through engineered crises and symbolic acts intended to force history into a sharper shape.

Archivists

Operate on monoliths. Drawn to monolith sites, incomplete archives, corrupted records, and the preservation or redirection of resonant meaning. Depending on the cell, members approach monoliths as sacred texts, exploitable instruments, historical treasures, or mechanisms to be controlled before others can reach them.

Syndicate

A durable criminal-commercial network that profits wherever order weakens. Trades in information, transport, salvage, black-market relics, smuggling, mercenary arrangements, and access. Operates with no particular ideology — instability pays.

Restorers

A hidden order operating independently across the cosmos. Not public knowledge. Do not coordinate with the Watchers.

Members hold that locating the Core Monolith — which they call the Mother of the Universe — would let them restore worlds, civilizations, and histories that have been lost. This belief drives their work on monolith lore, ruined worlds, erased archives, ritual theory, and hidden pathways through the Continuum.

Most Watchers do not know the Restorers exist. The upper echelon of the Order, and senior Bridge officers in the Fleet, hold fragmentary intelligence at best.

Runekeepers Working name

A small, hidden order on an unfindable world. Practitioners of runic transit — a discipline of marking celestial bodies in ways that attune the practitioner to that body, and through it, to the body's heart. Their existence in the wider cosmos is rumor at best.

Their homeworld defends itself by being unfindable; a ritual maintained by the order's senior practitioner withdraws the world from cosmological visibility. The world is real and is not lost — it is simply not discoverable by ordinary means. An exiled practitioner sent into the wider cosmos is given no map and no name; the order's custom is that home must be re-recognized through remembered sensory and cultural fragments.

Other arcane disciplines do not recognize what Runekeepers do as the same kind of work as Weave-based transit. The bypass of Forbiddance follows from this: the order's mode of travel is not Weave-based, and Forbiddance is specified against Weave-based teleportation. The deeper diagnostic is that a Wayfinder can only attune to bodies that have a heart in the cosmologically meaningful sense — a constructed installation does not qualify.

Wayfinders

Members of the order sent out into the wider cosmos for assigned purposes — to learn, to recover, to witness, or to bring something back. A Wayfinder carries a partial mark — the inscription their training has earned them — which grows as their understanding does. A Wayfinder with a complete mark can attune to a celestial body via ritual and time, opening a transit route between attuned points.

External Powers

The Rock of Bral, merchant powers, mercane traders, private captains, local governments, and world-level authorities. Most do not understand the Prime Continuum in any structural sense. They control lanes, supplies, politics, cover stories, contracts, rumors, and access — practical powers that frequently determine whether an operation can be run at all.
