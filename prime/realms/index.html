Setting·Geography

Realms

The Wildspaces, the worlds within them, and the places on those worlds.

Realmspace

Toril's Wildspace. A populous, well-charted sphere on standard Fleet navigation maps. Travel within the sphere by spelljamming helm takes days between worlds. Phlogiston transit out of Realmspace is reserved for long-haul senior-crew operations.

Caelestis

A Fleet station in Realmspace. A single structure with two functional wings: the Academy wing, where cadets train and live, and the Fleet operations wing, where contracts are issued and operational vessels work. Cadet graduation is internal movement from the Academy wing to operations.

Staffed and run by the Caelestis Academy & Fleet. Mirt is its headmaster.

Security

Caelestis is under permanent Forbiddance, maintained by Saerthe Abyzine as senior caster. Teleportation in, out, or within the warded area does not function. The ward is renewed nightly across a 30-day cycle and tested monthly at the dock perimeters.

Authorized internal transit uses bound Teleportation Circle sigils linking a small number of fixed points (the Bridge, the Brig, the Sky Dock control house, a handful of operational nodes). Sigil sequences are issued by role on a need-to-know basis and rotated periodically. Saerthe maintains the registry. Cadets and most personnel never see a sigil.

The wards are standard Fleet security for installations of consequence.

The Brig — Bay of Restricted Inert Goods

Caelestis's high-security holding. A vault, in plain Fleet language, for objects whose cosmological character is unknown, unstable, or institutionally inconvenient to discuss. The acronym is bureaucratic. Access is heavily compartmentalized: the full inventory is known to a small handful at the Bridge level; individual handlers know what passes through their station, not what is held elsewhere. Routes to and from the Brig are themselves classified; couriers know only the next handoff. Teleportation does not function in or out (see Security).

Current holdings include the World Surveyor, catalogued as a navigational instrument of antique origin.

Simulation Deck

Where Trial by Fire simulations run. Pods, scenario controls, instructor stations. Run by Boatswain Tarto.

Weeping Goddess

The station's main refectory and informal social hub. Cadets, instructors, visiting personnel, dock workers, and Fleet staff all eat here. Petty Officer Winston Ryeback presides over the galley.

Sky Dock

The station's surface-facing ship dock. Where most cadet ship-handling exercises take place. Kip and Pik, two rock gnomes, run ship-anatomy drills and dock operations.

Sea Dock

The station's lower cargo dock. A working dock for bulk supply, maintenance, and salvage handling. Rindle Gearloft works here.

Spelljammer Nexus / Chart Room

Navigational training and reference. Star charts, sphere maps, registered route logs, ledger archives. Saerthe is sometimes present.

Stores

The station's supply office. Mr. Blip's domain. Issued kit, ration rotation, and miscellaneous logistics pass through here.

Cadet Quarters

Bunk space for the Academy's cadet wing. Communal, with a few windows onto the astral dark for the wings that face outward.

Gymnasium / Assault Course

Physical training facility. Used for fitness, combat drill, and obstacle assessment.

Mirt's Quarters

The headmaster's private quarters, on the Bridge level. Contains an extradimensional wardrobe of unknown manufacture; its interior space corresponds to a stellar location in Virenspace. Mirt has not disclosed the wardrobe's provenance.

Toril

The principal inhabited world of Realmspace and the sphere's namesake. A standard-class world by Fleet cataloguing: atmosphere, oceans, continents, varied civilizations. Diplomatic and trade contact with Caelestis is established but limited; the Fleet's standard practice on Toril is to operate discreetly and not advertise the wider cosmos to its surface populations.

H'Catha

A toroidal world in Realmspace — a hollow ring with surface on its inner and outer faces and a central void. Ruled by beholders.

Beholder society on H'Catha is structured around competing hives, each dominated by a central tyrant. Hierarchies are violent and unstable; alliances between hives shift on the scale of decades. Beholder patrols range broadly through H'Catha's surrounding space, screening incoming vessels and engaging anything they do not recognize or cannot intimidate. Non-beholder traffic to H'Catha is rare and dangerous.

The world's central feature is the Spire, a structure of unknown origin standing at one of the torus's notable points. Beholder culture treats the Spire as significant, though accounts of its function differ across hives. An adamantine meteor struck the Spire in recent record; the strike site remains.

Virenspace

A Wildspace beyond H'Catha. Sparsely documented in standard Fleet archives. The system contains a stellar feature corresponding to the destination encoded in an extradimensional wardrobe held in Caelestis; Mirt has identified the match as somewhere in the Viren system.

Long-form notes in the Wildspace Lore Bible.

Untethered

Places whose sphere is not catalogued, or that are deliberately unfindable.

The Hollow Working name

The site of the Reach — the world that attempted to touch the Axioms directly and did not survive the attempt. The civilization is gone. The world is gone in the functional sense; what remains is a planet-scale ruin held together by paracausal pressure — fragments arrested in mid-dispersion by the still-thin local cosmology, frozen in a long shatter. Surface walking is possible for an observer who could reach it.

The Reach-monolith stands at the site, anchored where the Zenith manifested. One of the oldest standing monoliths. Distorted by proximity to whatever the location has become.

Location: profoundly hidden. Not present on any current chart. The site appears to be extradimensional — at or inside something resembling a black hole, where the metaphysical weak point of the Reach and a literal gravitational scar coincide. Standard transit cannot reach it; ordinary navigation does not lead there.

Name: no canonical name in any institutional record. "The Hollow" is a working placeholder. The civilization's own name for itself is among the things the Reach-monolith preserves that no living reader can decode.

Aethris

A world that experienced a Zenith event in recent record. The Zenith manifested as the completion of a long-prepared ritual at the Prism Ascendant, a piece of architecture built by Aethris's dominant culture specifically for the event. The world's dominant religion is the worship of Tymora; the culture frames fortune as destiny and compresses fated outcomes into ritual.

Two named categories of person exist within Aethris's society in relation to the dominant religion:

The Fortuned — those born marked by Tymora's favor. Treated as the society's natural elite.

The Misfortuned — those born marked against Tymora's favor. Treated by the dominant culture as aberrations.

The Prism Ascendant remains standing after the Zenith. Its present function is not externally documented.

Ascension World Specifics pending

A world where Vecna once forced a local threshold and won — a small, personal ascension distinct from the canonical Sigil event. The world remains marked by it: terrain, atmosphere, surviving structures all show the residue of an event in which a single will dictated local reality.

Surface accessible. Sphere not catalogued.

Lunarfoot Working name

A halfling world. Hides itself cosmically — the world is a living thing that decides what reaches it and what does not. Its concealment is maintained by a single living practitioner, the Runekeeper, whose body bears the inscription that holds the ritual.

Children of Lunarfoot who are sent into the wider cosmos as Wayfinders are given no map and no name — the order's practice is to withhold both. A returning exile must identify the homeworld through remembered sensory and cultural fragments, not by coordinates or by name. This is doctrinal, not a gap in record-keeping.

The Lunarfoot Runekeepers (see Factions → Runekeepers) are the order that maintains the ritual.

Cairn Station Specifics pending

A station situated in the territory of an astral dreadnought — the apex predator of the Astral Sea, a titanic chain-tailed leviathan that hunts dead cosmic matter. The dreadnought is the principal environmental hazard of the location: vessels survive a visit by avoiding it, moving past it, or timing their approach to its patrol.

Sphere not catalogued.
