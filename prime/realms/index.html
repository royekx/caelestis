Layer 03·Geography

Realms

Where it lives. The Wildspaces, the worlds within them, and the places on those worlds. Cosmology says what is true; Campaign says what matters; this is the ground the story stands on. People live in Factions & Characters; this page is places and named things. Operational state lives in the trackers.

Running the Realms

Organized by geography: Wildspace → celestial body → locale. A Wildspace is a star system; within it are worlds and stations; within those are the specific places the crew visits. To find somewhere, start with its sphere.

What earns a page entry Only canon-relevant places live here. Every named or implied Wildspace lives in the Wildspace Tracker; promote one to the Wildspace Lore Bible when it becomes active, and give it a section here when it becomes a destination.
Untethered Some places aren't yet placed in a Wildspace — either because the sphere is undecided or because the location is deliberately unfindable. They live in Untethered at the bottom until they find a home.

Realmspace

Toril's Wildspace. The crew's current operating area — the geographic leash (see Campaign → Scope). The published canonical material for this sphere stands as backdrop; this campaign uses it as a junction more than a destination — see the Crossroads Principle.

Travel within Realmspace is fast — days between worlds via spelljamming helm. Between Realmspace and the rest of the Continuum is the phlogiston, reserved for senior-crew long-haul work (the rank-buys-range progression).

Caelestis Home base

The station. Home base — the campaign's broader headquarters and long-term operational base. A strategic platform and a symbolic one: where military readiness, exploration, cosmological mystery, and interworld obligation meet. Staffed by the Academy & Fleet.

Lore Bible correction The Citadel is not close to Caelestis. The prior framing has been retired — see Cosmology → The Core. Caelestis is a strategic node on its own merits, not a Citadel satellite.

Caelestis is a single station with two wings. The Academy wing is Mirt's domain: training, cadets, simulation deck, classrooms, the Spelljammer Nexus, the Weeping Goddess refectory — where the crew came up. The Fleet operations wing is the handler's domain: the contract board, the working docks, visiting captains; part base, part market. Graduation is crossing the station from school side to operations side — the threshold is internal, not a relocation; the crew just earns the doors.

Untethered

Places whose sphere is undecided, or that are deliberately unfindable. They wait here until they find a home — at which point they move up into their Wildspace.

Aethris — a completed manufactured Zenith Cosmic anchor

Sol's homeworld. A world that reached its own Zenith — at a moment the network encouraged, because the responding monolith lands exactly where Vecna's figure needs a mark. The Prism Ascendant is the architecture Aethris's society built for the Zenith itself (a Tymora-worship civilization compressing destiny into ritual). Vecna's role was selection and timing, not invention. Aethris did the hard work of approaching the threshold on its own; he made sure it crossed at the right moment. The monolith that responded now stands inside his figure. Sol is the one survivor, the loose end.

Standing here is standing inside what other worlds are being aimed toward. This is someone else deciding what's real, made permanent at a planetary scale. One of the two anchor worlds — the reachable places that show the crew their own possible future.

The Misfortuned
The category Sol was born into on Aethris — those whom the world's dominant Tymora-worship considered aberrations. A failed Zenith ritual was the trigger of his escape; his survival is the world's loose end.
The Fortuned
Sol's counterparts. The fortune-blessed Aethris elite. They commissioned the Crystalline Retrieval Construct after Sol's escape, to bring him back.
The Prism Ascendant
The architecture of the Zenith. Still standing. Function unclear at this scale; one of the things Aethris-as-destination will eventually reveal.
Sphere undecided Aethris hasn't been placed in a Wildspace yet. When you decide where it sits, move it up under that sphere.

The Ascension World Specifics pending Cosmic anchor

A reachable world where Vecna once forced a threshold and won, locally — a small, personal ascension (not the canonical Sigil event) that left the world a scar: where "who decides what's real" was decided permanently in one being's favor and everything else became a footnote to him.

Standing on it is standing inside the failure state — a local Eve of Ruin you can walk. This is Vecna deciding. Together with Aethris, it's the campaign's thesis made into ground the crew can stand on.

Build later Canon permits this — Vecna's ascension origins are deliberately diffuse, so we're filling a gap, not contradicting one. Hold specifics for Arc II-late / III. Should be namable on the charts now as an unmarked point that resonates wrong. Decide name, sphere, and what the local ascension did when the crew is approaching it.

Tumak's homeworld Unnamed by design

Hides itself cosmically. The world is a living thing that decides what reaches it and what does not. Tumak does not know its name — this is intentional and cultural, not a gap. The name is withheld from the exiled child to preserve the integrity of the journey. Tumak must identify home through remembered sensory fragments and cultural impressions rather than by name or direct coordinates.

The world's concealment is maintained by the Runekeeper, whose body bears the inscription. The current Lunarfoot Runekeeper is dimming, which is why Wan and Tu were both sent as Wayfinders — a hedge against catastrophe that creates its own Zenith risk if both return together. Convergence + Intensification at personal scale.

Full mechanical detail in the Wayfinder/Runekeeper mechanics doc. See also: the Tumak / The Becoming seed. Unfindable by design — stays in Untethered permanently as a feature, not a placeholder.

Cairn Station Open thread

A station the artifact's trail eventually leads to. Sits in the territory of an astral dreadnought — the apex predator of the Astral Sea, a titanic one-eyed chain-tailed leviathan that hunts the dead matter of the cosmos.

The dreadnought is the lock, not the boss. The crew does not fight it — it's the environmental pressure that makes Cairn a white-knuckle destination. You avoid it, slip past it, time your movement to its patrol, get in and out before the eye turns your way.

Why information is hidden there
If you want to stash a piece of a cosmos-ending working where no institution will stumble on it, you put it in a dreadnought's hunting ground. The monster is the lock. Nobody audits a place that eats auditors. "Cairn" reads right — a cairn is a marker over the dead, and this is a station perched in a boneyard.
Why it's thematically loaded
An astral dreadnought eats the dead matter of the cosmos — the Astral's mechanism for unmaking what's already ended. A god-corpse-eater squatting over the network's secret is the charter's question wearing teeth: who decides what's real? The thing that eats what's already been decided-against.
Open thread → tracked in Campaign What Cairn Station is in character — derelict boneyard-archive vs. inhabited outpost-in-the-shadow — is an unresolved design question, not a settled location fact. It lives as a pull-able thread in Campaign → Seeds. The place is here; the decision is there. Also undecided: which Wildspace Cairn sits in, and whether the dreadnought's presence is coincidence or something the network's working attracts.

Things in Play

Named objects that matter. Concrete things the crew has touched, holds, or is moving toward. People who matter are in Factions & Characters.

The Foundling The crew's ship

A hammerhead-class spelljammer. The crew's vessel for the Realmspace sortie onward. Cannot survive the H'Catha approach (beholder patrols) — disguise logic puts the crew on the tyrant ship instead. The Foundling waits at the system's edge, positioned to detour and double back. Its errand: collect Ezra, the Watcher-in-training.

Boogie's shard / The Fonains Monolith Already in play

The stone inside Boogie's nucleus matches the Fonains monolith fragment in the FIA archive — a piece of the only monolith ever destroyed. Tharizdun-corrupted. A corrupted Continuity shard embedded in a living plasmoid.

Quietly one of the most important objects in the entire campaign. The shard's existence is the proof-of-concept for removing a point from the figure — the late-game answer to "how do you stop a finished spell," sitting dormant in a PC's chest, sourced from another table's history. See Campaign → Back-burner.

He also carries a half-ship (V01) and a salvage logbook inside his chest. He is becoming a container for archival residue. His "urge to dance" is a Pulse triggered by the corrupted shard.

The H'Catha Meteor Threshold-resonant

Adamantine that struck the Spire on H'Catha. Retrieved as a fetch quest in Voyage 004. Already logged as threshold-resonant in the Fleet archive (FIA-007). Hums on the same frequency as Boogie's shard, Gregory's core, and the clockwork resonance-cores.

Held at three depths: surface (a meteor) · middle (a fragment of an Ortholith, shed or aimed) · deep (the strike was bait). Decide depth as the crew earns it. The crew handing it over at graduation triggers the reclassification beat — the breadcrumb that retroactively loads Arc I.

The artifact (the network's prize) Specifics pending

An artifact no one at Caelestis understood. Miscatalogued, shelved as a curio, lifted quietly by the network's saboteur via the bribed dock seam under cover of Vocath's grudge-operation. Points the network toward the next point on Vecna's figure.

Candidates (all fit "miscatalogued curio that finds or touches monoliths"): locator-lens, monolith-key, monolith-fragment. Lean: locator-lens for the no-railroad-rule safety. Full discussion at The Remaking → The Artifact.

The Latchling Sea Dock

The first anomaly Gregory's resonance locates. A paracausal entity — its change is sentience where its kind has none. Hidden at the Sea Dock by Rindle Gearloft, who is protecting it from the academy. Gregory's repair work on a latch may have been the local condition that let the ripple resolve into it.

The arc is the journey (obstacles) more than the destination (Gregory knows where). Two-way recognition when they meet. Reframes Gregory's own origin.

The transmutation sigil / eye-in-wheel mark

The mark on the sabotage plates and beneath the neogi's chitin. The visual rhyme that converts isolated incidents into a pattern. A subtle Vecna helm. Fleet Intelligence can't match it — the crew tracks it before any institution does. The pattern-engine seed.

The clockwork horrors & resonance-cores

Made things consuming made things to make more of themselves, with no concept that the consumed were ever someone. The destructive face of the Existence Axiom. Cores of resonance-attuned crystal — same supply line behind other incidents.

They are the exact inverse of Gregory's question. Where Gregory asks "is a made thing a person?", the horrors are reality answering no, it's inventory.

The disc-marked crate cache

At the Sky Dock. Where Vocath's grudge-loot pools — the visible thefts (Ryeback's pistol, restricted supplies, navigational charts). Cover for the quiet artifact-lift underneath. The crew found it and didn't open it.
