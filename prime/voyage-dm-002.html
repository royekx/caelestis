Previous Session

Orientation threw five strangers into a simulation they couldn't win, then put them through a full day of academy life — bunks, Sky Dock, the Spelljammer Nexus, the Gymnasium. A simple delivery errand to Mirt the Merciless ended in a creature attack in his quarters: a coordinated diversion while the academy was being robbed. Sol chose to preserve the last creature alive rather than kill it. Casey slipped alone into Mirt's wardrobe, saw an unfamiliar constellation, and heard a voice say Go there. Gregory felt something directional pulling at his core. Tumak is hiding the constellation shirt Casey scratched, along with Mirt's stolen Bag of Holding. Boogie pulled a half-missing model ship from inside his own chest.

Voyage 002 opens the morning after. For the first time since arriving, the day briefly opens before the academy calls them back in. A new cadet — Bartholomew — is being folded into the crew today.

Main Plot This Session

Morning — Bartholomew is assigned to this crew by Tarto and pointed to the Weeping Goddess. He arrives before the party and meets Miken at the only open table. Lunch — The crew filters in; the only seats open are at that table. Ryeback brings food himself and prompts Casey for a Stellar Reading. Miken's confidence wavers — the party can lift his spirits and earn his crew's goodwill for what comes later. Afternoon — Free roam. The cadets follow their own threads across Caelestis. Evening — Players choose how to close the day. Forward — A few days pass in brief montage. Routine academy life takes hold. Then a runner brings a summons to the Simulations Deck — Trial by Fire begins. The session expects to enter the simulation; it does not need to finish it.

Strong Start — Bartholomew & Tarto
Read Aloud — Open on Bartholomew

The Boatswain's post is quieter than the rest of the academy. She doesn't greet you when you enter — she finishes the line she's writing, sets the ledger aside, and looks at you with the kind of attention that measures without announcing itself. "You're a wilder." Not a question. "That changes nothing once you're aboard a crew. There's one you'll be training with." She names the Weeping Goddess and the lunch hour without looking up again. "Find them. Eat. Keep up." The conversation is over before you realize it started.

Beat 1 — Ryeback & Casey's Reading

Ryeback brings food himself, a tray balanced on one forearm. Drops bowls of gunpowder chowder with affectionate brusqueness — "Eat. All of you. Especially you — stiff breeze'd put you in the infirmary." Glances at Casey: "You. The hadozee. Barret was at the counter last night running his mouth about you. Said you read his birthday off the stars. Said you got it right." Hip against the table. "Do mine."

  • Casey activates Stellar Reading. Three points light over Ryeback: {5, 7, 8} — uniquely Sagittarius. Holiday sign: Feast of the Moon — "Walks unseen; between worlds."
  • When Casey delivers the reading, Ryeback goes still. Grin doesn't move. "Huh." Raps a knuckle on the table on his way back to the counter: "Bowl's on the house, hadozee. The rest of you owe me."
  • Reward: Casey has advantage on his next social roll with Ryeback.
  • If anyone brings up the thefts in his earshot during lunch, he'll grumble loudly about his stolen custom pistol — a casual nudge that the thefts hit instructors and staff, not just officers.
Beat 2 — Miken's Confidence

Miken is jittery about the next training exercise. He's already convinced himself he doesn't have what it takes. The party can lift his spirits — and earn his crew's goodwill — through any of the following (DC 12). One success is enough.

  • Words of Encouragement — DC 12 Cha (Persuasion). Direct reassurance.
  • Problem Solving — DC 12 Int (Arcana). Talk shop with him about spelljamming; redirect his mind.
  • Feel the Vibe — DC 12 Cha (Persuasion). Speak to his crewmates; get them to call him over and pull him back in.
  • If any check succeeds, Miken's spirits lift. Apply a small situational bonus when the crews next meet during Trial by Fire — at your discretion (advantage on the first hazard check, or a friendly rivalry instead of an antagonistic one).

Read Aloud — The Days That Followed

The days that followed felt unlike the first. The chaos that had introduced you to Caelestis gave way to something steadier — lectures in the Nexus, drills on the simulation deck, meals taken without incident, hands learning the shape of new tools. Bartholomew became a face you nodded to in the corridor. Miken passed you in line at the Goddess and smiled. Ryeback shouted you down for not eating enough. The thefts went quieter, though the corridors stayed full of officers. Above cadet clearance, things were happening. Below it, you were learning. Then, one morning, a runner found your crew at breakfast with a folded note in her hand. Boatswain Tarto requests your presence at the Simulations Deck.

Outcomes / Transitions
Time has passed. The crew feels settled. Caelestis feels lived-in.
Cut directly to the Simulations Deck. Trial by Fire begins.
Scene 6 Trial by Fire — Major Beats Expected Play · Likely Will Not Complete
Location
Simulations Deck — main hall; then simulation chamber
What's Happening
Expect to enter the simulation. Do not expect to finish it. Hard-stop at any clean beat. Sabotage saves for V003 regardless.
Briefing — Saerthe & Tarto

The crew arrives at the same time as another team of cadets, including Miken. Saerthe and Tarto run the briefing. Friendly competition between teams; both squads earn points scored by the officers. The exercise: crew a simulated hammerhead, plot a course, survive Wildspace, and locate a ship lost in an asteroid cluster.

Saerthe's aside. As Saerthe explains the simulation, he mentions — almost in passing — that "one of my own ships has gone missing this week. I believe it's part of the thefts." He says this without alarm, the way someone names a fact already settled in his own mind, but it lands hard if the players are listening. The thefts have escalated from items to vessels — and an instructor is openly connecting his loss to the larger pattern. If pressed, he won't say more in front of the other crew — only: "We'll discuss it when the time is right."

Role Assignment — Use Caelestis Crew Roles

Use the homebrew Bridge Crew Roles document. Cadet rank for everyone. Spelljammer is mandatory and one occupant only — must be a spellcaster. Captain is one occupant only. Boatswain, Gunner, Navigator, and Medical Officer can be multi-occupant. Cap discussion at 10 minutes. Encourage players to pick the role that fits their character, not the optimal pick.

Tarto's pass — for Sol. While the cadets sort roles, Tarto walks past Sol. Doesn't stop. Doesn't slow down. Just says, low and casual, "You're the one who kept the neogi alive." Beat. "Noted." Keeps walking. Not a reward. Not a speech. Just acknowledgment — she sees him. The convening proper is still queued, but Sol's player knows his choice landed somewhere.

Test of Mettle — Journey Beats
  • Takeoff. Spelljammer makes a DC 13 spellcasting ability check. First-try success = score boost. Second try auto-succeeds.
  • Challenge 1 — Plot a Course. Each character makes a DC 12 check (Captain: Wis/Cha, Shipmate/Gunner: Str/Dex, Spelljammer: Int/Wis). Half success = pass.
  • Challenge 2 — Electrical Storm. Giant space eels swarm the ship. Wildjammer ship combat using bulwark mechanics. BP first, then Hull. Boatswain restores BP at start of turn.
  • Challenge 3 — Asteroid Cluster. Navigation hazard. Group checks.
  • Challenge 4 — Ship-to-Ship. Simulated hostile vessel. Wildjammer combat — Spelljammer initiative shared with ship.
  • Help! — Miken's Team Adrift. Other team's ship is severely damaged after the battle. Miken waves for help. If the crew rescues them, score boost; if Miken's confidence was lifted at lunch, the rescue is easier and the relationship reads as friendly.
Hard Stops

Natural session-end points: after Role Assignment, after any single Challenge, mid-ship-combat (cliffhanger), or after the Help! rescue. Do not run Sabotage. Save that for Voyage 003 regardless of how far the session gets.

Outcomes / Transitions
Crew earns or loses score points based on performance. Score determines downstream rewards.
Miken either earns the crew's friendship or remains an awkward acquaintance.
Wherever play stops, V003 picks up. Sabotage is the cold open there.
Secrets & Clues
The academy thefts are targeted and coordinated, not opportunistic. Someone knew which rooms to hit and when to create a diversion.
The thefts have escalated to vessels. One of Saerthe's own ships has gone missing this week. The academy has not yet announced it publicly.
Sol's preservation of the creature is the only reason the investigation has anything concrete to work with. Officers know this — they have not yet thanked him for it.
The neogi were not acting on their own. They were bound — astrally collared by an outside hand. They carry phlogiston scent: they were transported in, not native to Caelestis. Whoever did this can compel a slaver species. (DM lore — surfaces in the deferred convening.)
A deeper layer of academy secrecy exists above cadet clearance. Watcher · Zenith · Monolith — surface only as fragments, only if the players pry. Never explained.
Fleet sometimes moves specialized personnel to field assignments, not just cargo. Do not reveal Ezra specifically — only imply.
Sol's misfortune has shape — inconvenient, awkward, but always useful. It reveals what should be hidden. Something is wrong with timing around him.
Sol may be scried tonight by the Crystalline Retrieval Construct. Wisdom save, no DC announced. Sol does not know he is being looked for.
Gregory's anomaly is at the Sea Dock, near cargo. Rindle Gearloft has found it. He is hiding it. The pull is directional, not random.
Mirt knows more than he is saying. The wardrobe is his. The Viren reference is his. He is not yet ready to discuss it. (Surfaces in the deferred convening.)

Look Ahead — Deferred Scenes
Deferred · Likely V003 Post-Sabotage

The Convening — Tarto, Mirt & Saerthe

Originally planned for V002, deferred to keep this session moving toward action. Best run after Sabotage in V003 — once the academy has visible reason to take the cadets seriously as witnesses and the theft pattern is undeniable. Mirt's personal attendance signals stakes. Saerthe watches. Tarto runs the room.

Structure when ready:

  • Recount — The crew walks through what happened in Mirt's quarters. Mirt asks pointed questions about the wardrobe, the timing, what the creatures did first. Does not volunteer information.
  • Sol's Recognition — When the story reaches the preservation choice, Mirt stops. "You kept it alive. That's the only reason we have anything to work with. Your name will be in the report." Reward: formal commendation on file; restricted-access pass to the lower decks signed by Tarto.
  • Investigate the Creature — Players examine the preserved specimen. One check each, unlock one piece of information per success:
    • Arcana (DC 13) — astrally collared; the neogi was compelled, not acting on its own will
    • Investigation (DC 13) — sigils etched into the chitin beneath the limb joints; a brand a neogi would not bear willingly — there is a higher power above it
    • Nature (DC 13) — anomalies in its physiology suggest it has been altered; whoever bound it has changed something fundamental
    • Medicine (DC 13) — venom in the mandibles is paralytic, calibrated; meant to hold Mirt, not kill him
    • Survival (DC 12) — phlogiston scent on the legs; transported in from outside Caelestis
  • The Larger Picture (if they pry) — Mirt: "This is not the first time something like this has tried to reach me." Saerthe, almost to herself: "A Watcher would have caught the neogi before it crossed our threshold." Tarto closes: "You'll be told what you need to know when you need to know it. Dismissed."

When to run it: After Sabotage. The crew has now been targeted in a simulation chamber — that is the second incident. Mirt convenes them not just as witnesses but as a pattern. The Watcher / Zenith / Monolith fragments land harder when the player can already see the shape forming.
