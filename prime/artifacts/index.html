Setting·Cosmologically Significant Objects

Artifacts

Named objects of cosmological weight — extant, lost, sought. Most belong to the Reach, scattered residue of an ancient attempt to touch the Axioms directly. Others may exist outside that origin. Status — held, in transit, missing, in enemy hands — lives in the Campaign Tracker; durable record lives here.

Running the Artifacts

Artifacts on this page are not magic items by D&D's usual definition. They are cosmologically significant objects — paracausal in origin, singular in effect, mostly understood only partially by whichever institution currently holds them. They have provenance, history, and stakes attached. They are not items the crew "buys at a level-appropriate shop."

Each entry below tracks: what the artifact is (cosmologically), what it appears to be (in-fiction surface), what it does (function in play), and what each major party would want from it (the crew, the network, Vecna, the institutions). Status and current custody live in the tracker; lore lives here.

Setting, not campaign Artifacts are setting-level — they exist in this multiverse regardless of which campaign is running. A one-shot, a different crew, a future arc: all can pull from the same artifact roster. The state of a given artifact in a campaign (where it is, who holds it, what they know about it) is campaign-specific; the artifact itself is reusable.

The iconic-items principle Famous D&D items — Hand and Eye of Vecna, Book of Vile Darkness, others — exist in this multiverse, but their fame was earned in a different era than the current crisis. They do not advance or impede The Remaking. Recognized power is suspicious here. Real power tends to wear a different costume. See Cosmology → Iconic items, recontextualized. Iconic items not central to current play are not catalogued in this pillar; they show up situationally and are recontextualized when they do.

The Reach Set

Artifacts derived from the residue of the Reach — the ancient attempt by a single civilization to touch the Axioms directly. Each is biased toward whichever Axiom its origin fragment brushed. The bias determines what each can do.

The number of artifacts in the set is not known. A small handful are believed to exist. Caelestis holds one (the World Surveyor). Vecna is believed to hold or to be hunting others; their identities, axes, and current locations are deferred until they enter play.

The World Surveyor Boundary-biased

A brass-and-glass projection device, roughly palm-to-fist sized, with a hinged case and an armillary-style internal frame. At its heart, mounted at the central node of the frame, is a fragment of Reach residue biased toward Boundary — the Axiom of distinction, of this world is itself.

The device is made. The fragment is not. Whoever built the casing was a finder, not a creator — they discovered the fragment, observed that it responded to proximity to planetary bodies in ways no ordinary stone does, and built around it an instrument that could express the response as a readable form. The work is exceptional; multiple hands across generations probably refined it. The current configuration may be centuries old or older.

Function

Activated near a planetary body, the device projects a holographic chart of the world below — a three-dimensional rendering surfacing structural and cosmological features the naked eye and ordinary instruments cannot perceive. Once per long rest, the user reading the chart gains the effect of Commune with Nature, reframed: the device is not casting the spell, it is letting the world speak through the fragment. Three facts about that world surface, drawn from what the world knows of itself.

The kind of information surfaced depends on the world being read. A young world says different things than an old one; a world hiding a paracausal truth says different things than one without one; a world close to its own Zenith says urgent things. The device's readings can feel inconsistent in early use because worlds are not uniform — the inconsistency is the world talking back. A reader who uses it regularly will learn to interpret the variation; that interpretation is itself a slow-burn arc.

Stipulations to decide at the table Specifics on what each kind of world surfaces (e.g. which three things the chart will reveal in a given setting) are DM-defined per location. Each world deserves a one-line "what the World Surveyor says here" before a reader brings it close. Reserve the strongest readings for the worlds where their revelation matters most.

Cosmological depths of reading

The device's readings carry information at multiple cosmological depths. A reader sees what their cosmological understanding allows them to see — the same activation produces the same data, but the meaning extracted depends on the reader.

Surface — the readable layer
Anyone with ordinary perception can read the chart's surface features: the world's hidden geography, structures the surface hides, presences and absences, places of resonance. The world's secret as a destination. A reader at this level can use the device as a portable exploratory instrument — find places worth investigating, hard dungeons, world-secrets. This is the immediate, in-fiction value of holding the device — and it is real.
Operational — the surveying layer
A reader with cosmological-operational understanding can extract patterns across multiple readings. Cross-referencing what the device says about world after world produces a survey: which worlds give strong responses, which weak, which carry resonant features that fit some larger pattern the reader is testing for. A target list, or a confirmation log.
Structural — the architectural layer
A reader with structural cosmological understanding can perceive what the device is actually reading — the heart-network connectivity itself. The relationships between worlds' hearts. Which hearts resonate with which. The architecture of cosmological connection that the surveys are only summing. This is the deepest reading the device permits, and only a few entities in the cosmology have the understanding to perform it.
Same data, different meanings The device hides nothing from any reader. The data is the data; the interpretation is what each reader brings. A reader who can only see the surface sees what is there; a reader who can read structure sees more in the same data. The housing produces readings. The heart determines how deep the readings can go. For why this matters — and who's currently reading at which depth — see The Remaking → The World Surveyor.

Status

Current custody
Held in the Brig at Caelestis. Catalogued as "navigational instrument, antique, of uncertain origin, exceptional rendering capability" — known-rare, partially understood by the institution holding it.
Provenance
Partial. The instrument has changed hands across known history more than once. The trail before the most recent custodian is murky — finders have generally chosen to obscure their possession of it. This pattern is itself a tell, and recurs with most artifacts of the set: people who learn what they have, tend to hide it.
The heart's nature — DM menu (deferred) The specific cosmological identity of the World Surveyor's central fragment is not committed in fiction. The grounding claim — Reach residue, biased toward Boundary — is established. Specifics are held for late-arc reveal. Candidates, ranked by current lean:
  1. A fragment of the dead world's own Boundary-signature — the part of the world that knew it was that world, scattered when the world broke and now resonant with any other world's equivalent self-knowledge. Lead candidate.
  2. A piece of an unmade world — kernel of something that approached existence but didn't cross.
  3. An organ of the Continuum itself, naturally produced and dislodged by the Reach event.
  4. A localized echo of the Core's archival function, miniaturized by accident.
Decide when the reveal serves the table.

A note on ethics

The World Surveyor — like every Reach-set artifact — is a made thing using residue that was not made for it. The Continuum did not sanction the extraction of the fragment or its housing. Whoever first did this was, in a real sense, doing something the cosmology did not consent to.

Institutions that have held instruments of this kind across generations have, broadly, chosen not to think about the implication. The Restorers would in their darker moments call them grave-goods. Any user benefits from an extraction they did not perform — but they are benefiting from it nonetheless. If the table ever wants to play with this question, the framing is here.

Other Reach artifacts Deferred

Other artifacts of the set exist. Their identities, Axiom-biases, current locations, and exact functions are deferred until each enters play. The structural reading is established: each will be biased toward a different Axiom (Convergence / Dissolution / Transformation, in some configuration); each will express its bias differently; each will have been built around by a different hand in a different era; they will not look or work alike. The set's coherence is functional, not aesthetic.

Vecna is believed to hold some, to be hunting others, and possibly to be unaware of one or more. The crew may eventually encounter a second. They do not need to know the set exists until the moment that matters.
