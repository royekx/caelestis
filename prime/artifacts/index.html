The World Surveyor

A brass-and-glass projection device, palm-to-fist sized, with a hinged case and an armillary-style internal frame. At its heart, mounted at the central node of the frame, is a fragment of Reach residue biased toward Boundary — the Axiom of distinction, of this world is itself.

The device is made; the fragment is not. Whoever built the casing was a finder, not a creator — they discovered the fragment, observed that it responded to proximity to planetary bodies in ways no ordinary stone does, and built around it an instrument that could express the response as a readable form.

Function

Activated near a planetary body, the device projects a holographic chart of the world below — a three-dimensional rendering surfacing structural and cosmological features the naked eye and ordinary instruments cannot perceive. Once per long rest, the user reading the chart gains the effect of Commune with Nature, reframed: the device is not casting the spell, it is letting the world speak through the fragment. Three facts about that world surface, drawn from what the world knows of itself.

The kind of information surfaced depends on the world being read. A young world says different things than an old one; a world hiding a paracausal truth says different things than one without one; a world close to its own Zenith says urgent things. The device's readings can feel inconsistent in early use because worlds are not uniform — the inconsistency is the world talking back.

Specifics on what each kind of world surfaces (e.g. which three things the chart will reveal in a given setting) are DM-defined per location.

Cosmological depths of reading

The device's readings carry information at multiple cosmological depths. A reader sees what their cosmological understanding allows them to see — the same activation produces the same data, but the meaning extracted depends on the reader.

Surface — the readable layer
Anyone with ordinary perception can read the chart's surface features: the world's hidden geography, structures the surface hides, presences and absences, places of resonance. A reader at this level can use the device as a portable exploratory instrument.
Operational — the surveying layer
A reader with cosmological-operational understanding can extract patterns across multiple readings. Cross-referencing what the device says about world after world produces a survey: which worlds give strong responses, which weak, which carry resonant features.
Structural — the architectural layer
A reader with structural cosmological understanding can perceive what the device is actually reading — the heart-network connectivity itself. Only a few entities in the cosmology have the understanding to perform this reading.

The device hides nothing from any reader. The data is the data; the interpretation is what each reader brings. For who's currently reading at which depth in this campaign, see The Remaking → The World Surveyor.

Status & provenance
Current custody
Held in the Brig at Caelestis. Catalogued as "navigational instrument, antique, of uncertain origin, exceptional rendering capability" — known-rare, partially understood by the institution holding it.
Provenance
Partial. The instrument has changed hands across known history more than once. The trail before the most recent custodian is murky — finders have generally chosen to obscure their possession of it. This pattern is itself a tell, and recurs with most artifacts of the set: people who learn what they have tend to hide it.
The heart's nature — DM menu (deferred)

The specific cosmological identity of the World Surveyor's central fragment is not committed in fiction. The grounding claim — Reach residue, biased toward Boundary — is established. Specifics are held for late-arc reveal. Candidates, ranked by current lean:

  1. A fragment of the dead world's own Boundary-signature — the part of the world that knew it was that world, scattered when the world broke and now resonant with any other world's equivalent self-knowledge. Lead candidate.
  2. A piece of an unmade world — kernel of something that approached existence but didn't cross.
  3. An organ of the Continuum itself, naturally produced and dislodged by the Reach event.
  4. A localized echo of the Core's archival function, miniaturized by accident.

Decide when the reveal serves the table.

A note on ethics

The World Surveyor — like every Reach-set artifact — is a made thing using residue that was not made for it. The Continuum did not sanction the extraction of the fragment or its housing. Whoever first did this was, in a real sense, doing something the cosmology did not consent to.

Institutions that have held instruments of this kind across generations have, broadly, chosen not to think about the implication. The Restorers would in their darker moments call them grave-goods. Any user benefits from an extraction they did not perform — but they are benefiting from it nonetheless.

Other Reach artifacts

Other artifacts of the set exist. Their identities, Axiom biases, current locations, and exact functions are deferred until each enters play.

What's known about the set

The structural reading is established: each will be biased toward a different Axiom (Convergence / Dissolution / Transformation, in some configuration); each will express its bias differently; each will have been built around by a different hand in a different era; they will not look or work alike. The set's coherence is functional, not aesthetic.

Vecna is believed to hold some, to be hunting others, and possibly to be unaware of one or more. The crew may eventually encounter a second. They do not need to know the set exists until the moment that matters.
