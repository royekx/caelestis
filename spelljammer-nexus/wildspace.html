What Wildspace Is

Wildspace is the medium through which spelljamming vessels travel between the worlds of a given sphere. It fills the interior of every crystal sphere — the space between a system's sun, its orbiting bodies, and the sphere wall. It is not empty. A diffuse magical energy permeates the void, enabling spelljammer helm operation and sustaining the air envelopes that keep crews alive. Vessels that lose their air envelope in Wildspace do not survive for long. The manual section covering air envelope maintenance and emergency procedures should be reviewed before any void operation.

Gravity

Gravity in Wildspace does not behave as it does at a planetary surface. Planetary bodies generate gravity fields that function normally within a limited radius. Beyond those fields — which is to say, for most of the time a vessel spends in transit — gravity runs parallel to the hull, pulling crew toward the nearest large flat surface at roughly the same strength as surface gravity.

In practice: a crew member on the upper deck and one on the lower deck are both standing "down" relative to their surface. Objects released over the side of a vessel fall away from the ship. The midpoint of small vessels experiences negligible gravity; loose items will float. Secure your equipment. Capsized cargo in the void cannot be retrieved conveniently.

Air Envelopes

Every mass of sufficient size in Wildspace sustains an air envelope — a bubble of breathable atmosphere extending from its surface. A spelljamming vessel's envelope is proportional to the ship's mass and refreshes itself as the vessel moves through the void. Stationary vessels in Wildspace will find their envelope degrading over time. Keep the ship moving on long holds.

When two ships make physical contact, their envelopes merge. This is not incidental: it is the mechanism by which boarding actions occur. Contact = merged atmosphere = traversable gap. Crews should treat any approaching vessel's intent as unknown until positively established. Allowing contact with an unidentified vessel is allowing a potential boarding action to begin.

Navigation

The spelljammer at the helm has an intuitive sense of the sphere's geometry — a broad directional feel for the space around the vessel. This supplements rather than replaces conventional navigation. Fine positioning, rendezvous calculations, and approach to specific destinations require charts, direct observation, and crew navigation skills. A helm is not a map.

Realmspace charts maintained by the Fleet cover inner system bodies with reasonable accuracy. Outer Realmspace coverage is less consistent. Regions beyond Realmspace require consultation with the Navigation Records, independent surveys, or firsthand scouting. Do not rely on inner-system chart quality when operating in outer or extra-sphere regions.

Conditions

Wildspace is not uniform. Asteroid fields, gravitational anomalies, debris clouds, void-adapted fauna, and the occupants of other vessels all create conditions that require response. Temperature beyond an envelope is extreme in both directions depending on proximity to the local primary. Radiation levels vary by position.

The most consistent operational hazard is other ships. Pirates, raiders, and hostile entities operate throughout Wildspace. Fleet doctrine for unknown vessel contact: establish identification at distance, maintain separation, assume nothing. Most void encounters are resolved before weapons are drawn. The ones that are not require a crew that knows what it is doing before the contact happens.
