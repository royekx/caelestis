What It Is

Wildspace is the void between celestial bodies — planets, moons, asteroid clusters, nebulae, and stranger things that don't fit those categories. It is not empty. It is suffused with a near-imperceptible magical energy that makes spelljamming possible and keeps things breathing where they shouldn't be able to.

The best comparison is the sea. Planets are islands. Wildspace is the water between them. You can sail it, die in it, get lost in it, and find things that have no business existing in it. The Academy teaches cadets the first three. The fourth tends to teach itself.

Crystal Spheres

Each solar system — sun, planets, moons, and all the space between them — is enclosed within a vast crystalline shell called a crystal sphere. The sphere containing Toril and its neighbours is called Realmspace. The Academy operates within it.

Crystal spheres are not infinite. They have boundaries. To leave one, a ship must find a portal in the sphere wall and pass through into what lies beyond.

The Phlogiston

Between crystal spheres flows the Phlogiston — a chaotic, luminous, highly flammable substance that fills the space outside any given sphere. Spelljamming ships can navigate the Phlogiston to travel between spheres. This takes time. It also takes nerve.

The Phlogiston has one notable property that the Academy emphasises: gods cannot reach into it. Divine spells fail there. Divine communication falls silent. Whether that is considered a feature or a problem depends considerably on which gods you have been praying to.

Do not light a fire in the Phlogiston. This is not a metaphor.

Spelljamming Ships & Helms

A spelljamming ship is any vessel fitted with a spelljammer helm — a magical seat that allows a spellcaster to pilot the ship through Wildspace. The helmsperson sits, concentrates, and the ship moves. Speed and manoeuvrability scale with the caster's ability. The helmsperson cannot cast other spells while seated. It is demanding work.

Ships come in many designs: the squat, powerful hammerhead (the Academy's training vessel of choice), the sleek squid ship favoured by pirates and independent crews, the lumbering galleon for cargo runs, and stranger configurations besides. Hull design matters for combat. The helm matters for everything else.

Air Envelopes

Every spelljamming ship carries its own bubble of breathable air — an air envelope — that extends a short distance from the hull. This is what allows crews to survive in the void. The envelope replenishes itself slowly as long as the ship is in motion.

When two ships make contact in Wildspace, their air envelopes merge. This is how boarding actions happen: a crew brings their ship close enough to press hulls, the air merges, and people start climbing over. The Academy's simulation began at exactly this moment. The cadets were not warned.

Encountered: Astral Reavers

During the Simulation Deck exercise, the crew faced a boarding action led by astral reavers — hostile humanoids described in Academy briefing materials as "irregular Wildspace threats of variable origin." Fast, aggressive, and operating in coordinated groups.

The simulation was designed to be unwinnable. The cadets were expected to hold their ground and learn what it costs to fight in Wildspace. They did. The lesson cost accordingly.
