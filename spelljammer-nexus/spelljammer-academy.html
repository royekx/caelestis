Cadet Welcome Brief — Spelljammer Corps, Caelestis Station Issued to all incoming cadets upon assignment
Assignment to Caelestis

You have been assigned to Caelestis Station, the primary training installation of the Spelljammer Corps. Caelestis is a Fleet station occupying a fixed position in Astral Space at the outer edge of Toril's orbital band — close enough to Realmspace proper for practical training in local Wildspace, distant enough to operate without interference from surface jurisdictions. Your time here is structured around a single objective: making you a functional member of a spelljamming crew.

We do not train soldiers. We do not train explorers. We train crew. The distinction matters. A soldier operates within a command structure directed at a specific enemy. An explorer operates largely autonomously toward a personal objective. A crew member operates interdependently with others toward a shared goal that will change with every mission. What Caelestis produces is people who can function in that third condition, under pressure, in the void, with strangers who will need to trust them.

The Corps and the Fleet

The Spelljammer Corps is the operational arm of the Fleet. The Fleet encompasses all spelljamming vessels, their crews, and the command infrastructure that coordinates them. The Corps is the specialised component concerned with active operations — reconnaissance, recovery, escort, interdiction, and response to Wildspace threats.

Cadets graduate as sailors in the Fleet. Assignment to the Corps is a separate process, occurring after initial service and dependent on aptitude, performance, and operational need. Pursue it if that is your ambition. Do not assume it. Complete your training first.

Structure of Training

Orientation covers the fundamentals: station layout, security levels, equipment issue, and the Nexus. You are responsible for knowing where you are allowed to go and acting accordingly. Security checkpoints throughout Caelestis are not procedural formalities. Failure to comply with a checkpoint challenge has one outcome, and it is listed clearly in every posted notice.

Following orientation, training moves through the simulation chambers, practical Wildspace exercises, and mission assignments of increasing complexity and independence. Each stage is designed to introduce conditions you have not encountered before operating in them operationally. The sequence is intentional. Do not skip ahead of it.

Instructors and officers are addressed by rank. Boatswain Tarto manages cadet operations directly. Questions about assignments, postings, and training schedule go to her office. Questions about the station itself, its history, or the broader Fleet go to the Nexus reference library, where you are now.

A Note on Expectations

Caelestis does not produce cadets who know everything about Wildspace. It produces cadets who know how to function when they don't. Space is large. Most of it has not been charted to Fleet standard. Most of the situations you will encounter aboard a working vessel are not covered by any manual currently in this library. What training gives you is a framework that holds when the situation outpaces the manual.

Hold the framework. The rest is experience.
