What the Crew Knows
  • Mirt the Merciless's quarters contain an extradimensional wardrobe — a space that shouldn't exist inside a wardrobe. It appears to have been created or gifted by someone named Zerathis.
  • The interior of the wardrobe contains a constellation that does not appear on any Fleet chart.
  • Casey entered the wardrobe during the Voyage 001 crate incident and heard a voice. The voice said something. That something was directional.
  • A star pattern matching the wardrobe's constellation was scratched onto a plain shirt. That shirt is currently in the possession of the crew — specifically, Tumak.
  • Zerathis appears to have connected the wardrobe specifically to the Viren system — a location that lies beyond H'Catha, outside standard Fleet navigation routes.
  • Gregory felt a directional pull during orientation. He has not said from what.
What the Name Refers To

The Viren system appears on no Fleet chart. It is not a registered destination in the Spelljammer Nexus library or the Sky Dock routing ledgers. The name itself came through the wardrobe encounter — it was not provided by Academy staff or orientation materials.

Whether "Viren" refers to a star, a system, a location within a system, or something else is not yet established. The constellation on the shirt is the only physical reference the crew currently holds.

Open Questions

Who is Zerathis, and what is their connection to Mirt. What the voice said, and what "go there" means in practical terms. Why a navigational constellation from an uncharted system is hidden inside a merchant's wardrobe at a Spelljammer Academy. Whether the pull Gregory felt is related. Whether the thefts — specifically the stolen navigational charts — are connected to whoever already knew where to look.

The shirt exists. The wardrobe exists. The constellation is real enough to scratch onto cloth. Someone knew about this system before the crew did.
