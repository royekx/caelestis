The Nature of Crystal Spheres

Each known system of worlds exists within a crystalline shell of enormous scale, referred to uniformly as a crystal sphere. The sphere encloses everything within it: the primary sun or suns, all orbiting bodies, all Wildspace between them, and any station or installation placed within its bounds. Caelestis Station, for instance, sits within the sphere enclosing Toril and its neighbours — the sphere designated Realmspace in Fleet records.

The sphere wall is, for practical purposes, impenetrable. No known conventional force can breach it. A vessel that runs into the sphere wall at any speed short of spelljamming transit will find itself stopped by a surface that gives nothing. The only passages through the sphere wall are naturally occurring portals — openings that allow a vessel moving at spelljamming speed to pass from Wildspace into the medium beyond.

Portal positions shift. Well-trafficked spheres like Realmspace have established portal charts maintained by Fleet navigators, and major portals have been stable for centuries of recorded survey. Less-charted spheres may require active search to locate transit points. Refer to Navigation Records for current Realmspace portal data.

The Phlogiston

Beyond the sphere wall lies the Phlogiston — the medium that fills the space between all known crystal spheres. It is not Wildspace. It is not vacuum. It is a turbulent, luminous substance that has been described by those who have sailed it as beautiful, disorienting, and relentlessly hostile to the unprepared.

Every navigator bound for inter-sphere travel must understand two properties of the Phlogiston without exception.

The first is combustibility. The Phlogiston ignites. Open flame in the Flow does not produce a contained fire — it produces a catastrophe. Fleet vessels in Phlogiston transit operate with all external flame sources sealed or extinguished. The heating and cooking arrangements aboard vessels are specifically designed for Flow operation. Any breach of this protocol risks the loss of the ship. This is recorded here as information, not as emphasis. The emphasis belongs to whoever is responsible for fire discipline aboard your vessel.

The second is divine silence. In the Phlogiston, the connection between a mortal and their god is severed. Divine spells fail. Clerical contact with higher powers does not reach. The mechanism is not agreed upon — it has been the subject of scholarly argument for as long as there has been inter-sphere travel — but the effect is consistent. A paladin in the Flow is a paladin without their patron. Plan accordingly.

Transit Procedure

Passing through a sphere portal requires spelljamming speed. The vessel must approach at heading, at the correct velocity, and the portal must be present. When those conditions are met, transit is instantaneous — a membrane, not a barrier. The vessel enters the Phlogiston as if the sphere wall were not there. The star field gives way to the churning colour of the Flow.

Re-entry to a sphere follows the same process in reverse, approached from the Phlogiston side. Locating a portal from outside a sphere requires either current chart data or an extended search pattern. Entering a poorly-charted sphere without portal reference data is a navigational commitment the Fleet does not recommend lightly.
