Site Profile — Inferred

At decommissioning, the structure was reported intact and operationally serviceable. No demolition order is on file. Cause of decommissioning is not legible in the records available at this clearance.

It is not a dead drop. Personnel are likely present during transfer windows. Whether continuous garrison is maintained between windows is not established.

Approach Considerations

Three approach vectors are inferable from the chart in Case #LG-008:

· Direct approach — assumes successful recognition-protocol bluff at primary gate. Fastest contact. Highest risk of immediate hostile response if bluff fails.
· Stealth approach — exploits the debris zone for cover, requires extended transit and a vessel profile small enough to evade perimeter sensing. Sets up boarding rather than docking.
· Stand-off observation — establishes pattern of life before committing. Lowest risk, longest commitment. Recommended if a transfer window can be identified by external means (intercept of courier traffic, second LORN-equivalent contact, etc.).

Vessel-class recommendation: squid ship or damselfly equivalent. A hammerhead profile is unsuitable; the approach geometry does not reward armament over manoeuvre.

Risk Assessment — Final

This is not a safe recovery. The cache is active. The recognition protocol is a one-time bluff at best. The defenders, by composition and by the materials they are holding, are not a fight a cadet-tier crew can win on superior force.

The recovery becomes viable only if the crew has: the protocol, a small fast vessel, an experienced helm, and the discipline not to escalate contact unless the situation requires it. Anything else and Fleet recommends standing off.
