# FIA Breadcrumbs — drafts to paste into the player-facing Fleet Intelligence Archive

These are subtle reward-the-reader entries. They go on the **player-facing FIA site**
(outside `/prime/`). Each is a single line or short paragraph that establishes a
category of thing — the Brig — without revealing its function or its contents.

A player who reads the FIA carefully and remembers the word *"Brig"* gets a small
recognition moment when, later, they hear it again in a different context (a fleeing
thief, an overheard officer, a redacted report). Foreknowledge as social capital at
the table. Doesn't solve any puzzle. Just rewards attention.

---

## Entry 1 — General reference to the Brig

Suggested placement: in any FIA entry describing Caelestis's institutional
structure, security posture, or restricted facilities. A bureaucratic side-note,
not a feature.

> Restricted holdings aboard Caelestis are managed by the Brig — the Bay of
> Restricted Inert Goods — under separate clearance. Inquiries regarding Brig
> inventory, transit, or access must be routed through Bridge oversight; direct
> approach to Brig personnel is prohibited per standing order. The Brig's
> physical location and access protocols are themselves classified.

Voice: institutional, clipped, the standard FIA register. Doesn't say what the
Brig holds; just establishes that it *exists* as a category. The word *"Inert"*
is the comforting fiction — the player who later realizes nothing in the Brig is
actually inert gets to feel that the institution was lying to them all along.

---

## Entry 2 — Incident-pattern analysis with Brig liaison

Suggested placement: append to an existing FIA entry that discusses the Academy
thefts, the sabotage pattern, the neogi brand, or any of the surveillance-cover
incidents. The line should arrive as the closing note of an investigation log,
where the academy's own analysts have started suspecting something larger.

> Pattern-of-incident analysis (cover thefts, sabotage plates, courier-routing
> anomalies) flagged for review by Brig liaison. Frequency and distribution
> suggest reconnaissance-pattern rather than opportunistic theft. Forwarded
> under restricted advisory.

Voice: investigation-log dry. The implication, to a reader who notices: the
academy itself has quietly connected the cover thefts to vault-related concern.
The institution is one step behind, but it's not asleep. The crew, if they
remember this, will know — when the thief comes haggard out of the Brig at
graduation — that the academy was waiting for something like this.

---

## What NOT to add to the FIA

These are tempting and should be resisted, because each spoils more than it
rewards:

- A description of what's *in* the Brig. Hand-wave the contents; never list.
- A mention of "the World Surveyor" or any specific artifact by name. The
  artifact's identity is the heist's reveal; pre-naming it removes that beat.
- A reference to Vecna, the network, or the Reach. None of these are FIA-readable
  intelligence yet. The FIA reports facts about *incidents*, not theories about
  *coherent enemy projects.* Stay in the institutional voice.

---

## Tone reminder

The FIA is Fleet Intelligence speaking to Fleet personnel. It is dry, factual,
slightly bureaucratic, occasionally clipped. It is *not* mysterious or
suggestive. The mystery comes from the **reader noticing patterns the FIA
hasn't yet connected itself.** That's the whole game. Write each entry as if
its author is mildly puzzled but professionally restrained.
