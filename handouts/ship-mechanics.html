Ship Mechanics
Flying, fighting, and surviving in Wildspace.
Your Ship
Armor Class
What attackers need to hit the ship. Same as creature AC.
Hull Points
The ship's health. Reaches 0 and the ship stops functioning. Hard to repair mid-combat.
Bulwark Points
A damage buffer in front of Hull Points. Damage hits BP first. Easier to recover than HP.
Speed
How far the ship moves per turn in combat (in feet).
Agility
How far the ship can turn per turn, in degrees. Higher = more maneuverable.
Crew
Sailors manning the ship. Fewer crew means fewer weapon attacks.
How Bulwark Works All incoming damage depletes Bulwark Points first. Hull only takes damage once BP reaches 0. Your Boatswain passively restores BP each turn — but a Fire critical stops that recovery until they spend an action to extinguish it.

Combat
Your Turn
You roll and act normally. All your character's actions and abilities still work.
Your Role
Your bridge crew role gives you additional special actions — not instead of your normal ones.
Ship's Turn
The ship acts at the Spelljammer's initiative. They direct movement, turning, and weapon fire without spending their own actions.
Switch Role
Switching to a vacant role costs a bonus action. Single-occupant roles require the current occupant to agree.
What You Can Target
The Ship

Deals damage to BP then Hull. The direct approach.

Weapons

Destroy a weapon to silence it. Each has its own HP.

Crew

Fewer crew = fewer weapon attacks the ship can make.

Officers

Individually tracked enemies. Taking them out has big impact.

Ship Weapon Attacks
d20 + Proficiency + Str or Dex
Your choice of modifier on the attack roll.
Damage — no modifier added
Roll the weapon's damage dice only. Ability modifier is not added to damage.
Crew requirement. Each weapon needs a minimum number of crew to fire. No crew available = can't fire. Gunners at Officer rank reduce this by 1.
One fire per turn. A weapon can only be fired once per turn. A crew member can only man one weapon per turn.
Class features. Features like Extra Attack or Divine Smite can apply to ship weapons — but only once you reach Officer rank as a Gunner. Check with your DM for specifics.

Boarding
Within 5 ft. Just move across. No action required — treat it like stepping through a doorway.
Grappling hook or plank. Either requires an action to set up, then you can cross.
Jump, fly, teleport. Any movement available to your character works normally. Use the Ready action if waiting for the Spelljammer to close distance.
Captain's Boarding Party. At Officer rank, the Captain can call a coordinated boarding — everyone who joins swings across simultaneously at the start of the next round.
✦ ✦ ✦
Hazards
What they are. Non-combat challenges — asteroid clusters, interstellar fog, fire on board, electrical fields. Each is a group skill check.
How they work. Each character contributes one check. Your bridge crew role shapes which options are available. The crew makes a single roll too. Majority success or failure determines the outcome across four tiers.
Be creative. The listed checks are suggestions. If you have a better idea for how your character would help, pitch it to the DM.
