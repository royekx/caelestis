The Simulation
  • Cadets woke on the deck of the Moonraider mid-battle, no warning — Captain Sardax bleeding, reavers over the rail
  • Gregory hid and survived; Tumak took heavy fire but held; Sol planted himself at a ballista and killed an attacker; Casey fought and went defensive with cold magic; Boogie moved toward Tumak to help
  • Boatswain Tarto froze the illusion and revealed it: the memory of Captain Sardax's real last battle. A test with no winning condition. Courage mattered. So did knowing when to retreat.
  • Tarto called out each cadet's best moment — Casey's spellwork, Gregory's quick thinking, Sol's battlefield control, Tumak's toughness, Boogie's instinct to help over glory
Orientation
  • Administration: Met Sorcur (thri-kreen, telepathic). Received packets, vouchers, temporary access stars. Caelestis is a machine of access and hierarchy.
  • Cadet Quarters: Another cadet, Vena, tried to take Boogie's corner bunk (clear view into the Astral). Boogie held his ground; Sol moved close to back him. Vena backed off. First moment of the crew taking each other's side.
  • Sky Dock: Tour with Kip and Pik (gnomes who speak like one mind). Learned helm basics: ship and spellcaster become one motion. Boogie spotted a suspicious exchange between a dock officer and a merchant.
  • Quartermaster (Mr. Blip): Weapons and gear issued. Gregory visibly unsettled by Mr. Blip — an autognome who looks too much like him. Sol talked his way into heavier arms. Boogie charmed a light hammer out of Mr. Blip. Thinking about repair, not command.
Spelljammer Nexus
  • Arrived during an ectoplasmic breach elsewhere in the tower (tension noted)
  • Saerth Abyzine ran the helm exercise: guide a miniature ship over a floating field using mind and magic
  • Boogie took to it immediately — motion at a distance is natural to a body made of shifting form
  • Casey struggled until he stopped forcing it and found his own posture
  • Gregory kept thinking to the destination before the method — Saerth named it, Gregory adjusted
  • Sol navigated by anticipating danger before it arrived
  • Tumak managed — Saerth noted a divided quality, as if part of his mind was always elsewhere
  • Afterward, Boogie said it aloud: he doesn't want the helm. He wants to keep the ship alive.
Gymnasium
  • Assault course: false explosions, electrified water, swinging blades, final bell climb
  • Gregory — most hesitant in the first battle — was the one who saw through the first illusion and called it to Sol
  • Sol and Tumak both paid in pain for missteps but refused to stay down
  • Casey crossed sections with ease — born to leap and climb
  • Sol fell hard enough to be dragged aside by automatons; forced himself back up after Boogie shouted him onward. Boogie then used magic to help Gregory on the final ascent.
  • Boatswain's assessment: "The lag between you is good. You're starting to move as a crew, not five people sharing the same danger."
The Mirt Errand
  • Tasked to deliver a crate to Mirt the Merciless (academy head) in bridge quarters before lunch
  • En route: Tumak asked about origins. Boogie's answer was sad — drifting, borrowed identities, no family, loneliness. Told Tumak: "Now we have a crew. The crew will help you find them."
  • Sol and Casey carried the crate. Some talking, some carrying, all moving the same direction.
  • Mirt's room: Hungover, cluttered, a fishbowl with two octopi (Flotsam and Jetsam). Wardrobe door opening onto Astral night — a floor you could stand on, stars where walls should be.
  • The crate released worm-like centipede creatures. Fight. Sol subdued the last one alive rather than kill it — correctly read it might matter as evidence. Mirt praised the call.
  • Mirt rewarded them with gold. Told Casey privately: the wardrobe is a magical replica of a place beyond Hakatha in the Viren star system, gifted by a spellcaster named Zerathis.
  • Mr. Blip arrived with officers: the attack on Mirt was a diversion. Multiple burglaries across the academy happened simultaneously.
End of Day
  • Tumak hung curtains around his bunk — carved out privacy. Hid the marked shirt Casey passed him without explanation.
  • Casey slipped into the wardrobe, saw unfamiliar constellations, heard a voice say "Go there." Stole a shirt marked with what he'd seen. Later passed it to Tumak.
  • Gregory felt a new pull in his core — directional, toward somewhere unknown.
  • Sol when asked why he came here: "My life always turned unlucky. I wanted to spend it exploring anyway."
  • Boogie reached into his own chest and pulled out a tiny model spelljamming ship — the same kind from training. Half of it was missing. No explanation for how it got there.
